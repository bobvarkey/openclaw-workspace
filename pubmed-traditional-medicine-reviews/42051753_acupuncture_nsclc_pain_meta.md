# Efficacy and Safety of Acupuncture for Pain in Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer: A Meta-Analysis

**PMID:** 42051753  
**DOI:** 10.2147/JPR.S588869  
**Journal:** Journal of Pain Research  
**Published:** 2026-04-29  

## Authors
- Liu J, Chang J, Ye Y, Deng Y, Zhou H, Li X — *Guangzhou University of Chinese Medicine*  
- Zhang H — *Guangdong Provincial Hospital of Traditional Chinese Medicine*

## Keywords
`acupuncture` · `efficacy` · `non-small cell lung cancer` · `safety`

## Abstract
A meta-analysis of **11 randomized controlled trials (745 NSCLC patients)** found acupuncture significantly outperformed medical treatment in:
- **Pain reduction** (SMD = −1.33, 95% CI [−2.02, −0.63])
- **Adverse event incidence** (OR = 0.25, 95% CI [0.14, 0.43])
- **Total effective rate** (OR = 3.30, 95% CI [1.15, 9.46])

Acupuncture also improved anorexia, insomnia, and fatigue symptoms vs. medical treatment alone. Sensitivity analyses validated robustness; no significant publication bias detected.

**Conclusion:** Acupuncture demonstrates positive effects for cancer pain in NSCLC patients with a favorable safety profile.
