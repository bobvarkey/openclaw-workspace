# Effect of Traditional Chinese Medicine Non-Pharmacological Interventions on Cognitive Function in Mild Cognitive Impairment: A Systematic Review and Network Meta-Analysis

**PMID:** 42051696  
**DOI:** 10.3389/fmed.2026.1799759  
**Journal:** Frontiers in Medicine  
**Published:** 2026-04-29  

## Authors
- Dong Z, Li P, Xu J, Wang S, Li Z, Yang N, Zhang Z, Wu X — *Changchun University of Chinese Medicine*

## Keywords
`cognitive function` · `mild cognitive impairment` · `network meta-analysis` · `systematic review` · `traditional Chinese medicine non-pharmacological interventions`

## Abstract
Network meta-analysis of **76 RCTs (4,895 MCI patients)** comparing **9 types of TCM non-pharmacological interventions**:

**MMSE Score Improvements (top 3):**
1. **Physical and mental exercise (MBE)** — MD = 2.18, 95% CrI: 0.86–3.55 (SUCRA: 82.56%)
2. **Massage (TN)**
3. **Acupoint catgut embedding (ACE)**

**MoCA Score Improvements (top 3):**
1. **TEAS** — MD = 3.31, 95% CrI: 1.87–4.75 (SUCRA: 88.16%)
2. **Massage (TN)**
3. **Acupoint catgut embedding (ACE)**

**Registration:** PROSPERO CRD420251248307
