# Shuxuening Injection (Ginkgo Biloba Leaf Extract) for Vascular Cognitive Impairment: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis

**PMID:** 42051256  
**DOI:** 10.3389/fphar.2026.1720444  
**Journal:** Frontiers in Pharmacology  
**Published:** 2026-04-29  

## Authors
- Miao J, Liu Y, Feng H, Zan S, Miao C, Wang J, Jin X, Yang F, Pang W — *Tianjin University of Traditional Chinese Medicine*

## Keywords
`Ginkgo biloba leaf extract injection` · `Shuxuening injection` · `meta-analysis` · `systematic review` · `vascular cognitive impairment`

## Abstract
Meta-analysis of **22 trials (n = 2,375)** conducted in China (2005–2025). SXNI (Ginkgo biloba extract) added to conventional treatment vs. conventional treatment alone:

| Outcome | Effect Size |
|---------|-------------|
| MMSE | MD = 3.61, 95% CI 3.06–4.17 |
| HDS | MD = 1.30, 95% CI 0.21–2.39 |
| Barthel Index | MD = 9.06, 95% CI 4.66–13.45 |
| Overall response rate | RR = 1.27, 95% CI 1.21–1.33 |
| NIHSS | MD = −6.17, 95% CI −7.90 to −4.45 |
| Adverse events | RR = 0.72, 95% CI 0.36–1.44 (NS) |

**Conclusion:** SXNI shows cognitive and neurological benefits with no significant increase in adverse events. Evidence certainty is limited by methodological weaknesses.
