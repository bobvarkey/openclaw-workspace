# VZV Myelitis Research Notes

**Date:** 2026-03-23

## Key Paper
**Varicella zoster virus-related myelitis: a case series and literature review**
- PMID: 41794791
- Virology Journal, Mar 2026

## Summary
VZV remains latent in dorsal root ganglia → reactivates as shingles. In immunocompromised → encephalitis, meningitis, myelitis, neuropathy.

## Case Series (3 patients)
- 49F, 60M, 56M — none had typical rash
- **Cases 1 & 2:** Encephalomyelitis at onset — fever, impaired consciousness, long-segment spinal cord lesions
  - CSF mNGS detected VZV nucleic acid
- **Case 3:** VZVM + cerebral venous sinus thrombosis → delayed thoracic myelitis 40 days later (CSF mNGS negative)

## Treatment
- All: IV antiviral therapy
- 2/3: Corticosteroids + IVIG
- 1/3: IV methylprednisolone

## Outcomes
- 1/3: Full motor recovery
- 2/3: Remained paraplegic

## Key Takeaways
- Can occur WITHOUT rash
- Can be acute OR delayed (40 days)
- Early MRI + CSF mNGS = critical
- Prompt antivirals + immunomodulation → better outcomes
