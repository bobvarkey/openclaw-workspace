# Blood Clot Risk & HRT — Summary

## Summary

VTE risk varies dramatically by HRT preparation. Transdermal estradiol + micronized progesterone carries no increased VTE risk (baseline 2–10/10,000). Oral preparations (Prempro: 53/10,000) significantly increase risk. Modern HRT uses transdermal routes — WHI study findings do not apply.

## Key Risk Numbers (per 10,000 women-years)

| Preparation | VTE Risk |
|------------|:--------:|
| Baseline | 2–10 |
| Transdermal E2 + micronized P4 | **No increase** |
| Oral estradiol + micronized P4 | 37 |
| Prempro (CEE/MPA) | 53 |
| Pregnancy (3rd trimester) | 114 |
| Postpartum | 421 |

## Clinical Pearl

**WHI ≠ Modern HRT.** WHI used oral Prempro. Current standard of care is transdermal estradiol + micronized progesterone — different pharmacokinetics, different risk profile.
