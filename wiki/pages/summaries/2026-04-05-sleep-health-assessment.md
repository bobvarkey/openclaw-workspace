# Sleep Health Assessment Summary

## Summary

A sleep health assessment report based on AAFP 2024 and VA/DOD 2025 guidelines. The subject shows moderate clinical insomnia (ISI 19/28), low OSA risk (STOP 1/4), mild anxiety (GAD-7 7/21), mild depression (PHQ-9 9/27) with a safety alert for positive self-harm screening. First-line treatment is CBT-I.

## Key Findings

| Assessment | Score | Severity | Action |
|------------|-------|----------|--------|
| ISI (Insomnia) | 19/28 | Moderate | CBT-I recommended |
| STOP (OSA) | 1/4 | Low risk | Continue insomnia eval |
| GAD-7 (Anxiety) | 7/21 | Mild | Watchful waiting |
| PHQ-9 (Depression) | 9/27 | Mild | ⚠️ Safety alert — self-harm item positive |

## Risk Factors Identified
- Chronic duration: 6–12 months
- Nightly occurrence (6–7x/week)
- Screen use before bed
- Excessive time in bed

## Treatment Hierarchy

**First-line:** CBT-I (gold standard) or BBT-I
**Adjunct:** Short-term pharmacotherapy, MBSR, exercise
**Avoided:** Benzodiazepines, diphenhydramine, trazodone, antipsychotics, sleep hygiene alone
