# Diaphragm Atrophy — Summary

## Key Findings
- **Left hemidiaphragm**: Severely atrophic (0.10 cm)
- **Thickening fraction**: 0% (no contraction)
- Indicates **diaphragm paralysis**

## Interpretation
Normal diaphragm: >30% thickening fraction. 0% = complete paralysis. Thickness <0.15 cm = severe atrophy.

## Causes
Phrenic nerve injury, ALS, Guillain-Barré, post-surgical.
