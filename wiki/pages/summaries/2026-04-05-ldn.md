# Low Dose Naltrexone (LDN) — Summary

## Drug
Lodonak 1.5 mg — Low Dose Naltrexone Hydrochloride

## Mechanism
At low doses (1.5–4.5 mg): transient opioid blockade → upregulated endogenous opioids → increased endorphins + anti-inflammatory TLR4 modulation

## Uses
Fibromyalgia, MS, Crohn's disease, chronic pain (mixed evidence)

## Key Points
- Contraindicated with opioid medications (precipitates withdrawal)
- Taken at bedtime
- Not FDA-approved for most uses
