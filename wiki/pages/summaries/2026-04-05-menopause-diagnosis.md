# Menopause Diagnosis — Summary

## Key Point

**45–55 year old + 12 months amenorrhea + vasomotor symptoms = Clinical diagnosis. No labs needed.**

## When to Test

| Situation | Indication |
|-----------|------------|
| Age <40 | Suspected POI — confirm with FSH |
| On hormonal contraception | Masked symptoms |
| Post-hysterectomy | No menstrual cue |
| Atypical | Rule out alternatives |

## Lab Markers

| Marker | Threshold |
|--------|-----------|
| FSH | >40 IU/L |
| Estradiol | <20–30 pg/mL |
| AMH | Low/undetectable |

**POI rule**: FSH >40 on **two occasions, 4–6 weeks apart**

## Clinical Pearl

Perimenopausal FSH fluctuates wildly — clinical judgment trumps labs. Always rule out thyroid/prolactin issues.
