# SFEMG vs CNE Needle Comparison

## Summary

Technical comparison of single fibre EMG (SFEMG) vs concentric needle electrodes (CNE) for jitter studies. CNE is increasingly preferred due to lower cost, disposability, and acceptable jitter measurements — though values differ from true SFEMG.

## Key Differences

| Parameter | SFEMG | CNE |
|-----------|:-----:|:---:|
| LFF | 500 Hz | 1 kHz |
| Sweep | 0.5 ms/div | 1 ms/div |
| Gain | 200 µV | 50 µV |
| Amplitude | 200 µV | ≥50 µV |
| Rise time | <300 µs | <300 µs |

## Key Takeaway

**CNE jitter values are less than SFEMG values** — reference values from SFEMG studies cannot be directly applied to CNE studies.
