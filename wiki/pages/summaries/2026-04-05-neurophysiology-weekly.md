# Clinical Neurophysiology & Sleep Medicine — Weekly Summary

## Summary

Three studies advancing nerve conduction study (NCS) practice: a practical electrode modification for hypoglossal NCS, age/sex-stratified phrenic nerve norms, and a multi-agent AI system achieving 92% accuracy in EMG/NCS interpretation.

## Key Findings

| Study | Topic | Key Result |
|-------|-------|------------|
| Oliveira 2026 | Hypoglossal NCS | Submental electrodes match tongue electrode norms (latency ≤1.58 ms, amplitude >0.88 mV) |
| Castro 2026 | Phrenic NCS | Age-adjusted norms: latency +0.25 ms/decade; sex differences significant |
| Gorenshtein 2025 | AI in EDX | Multi-agent INSPIRE: 92.2% accuracy vs 62.6% base LLM |

## Clinical Implications

- **Hypoglossal NCS**: Submental placement eliminates tongue electrode complexity without sacrificing data quality
- **Phrenic NCS**: Age/sex stratification is essential — men have higher amplitudes, left side is larger
- **AI in EDX**: Multi-agent架构 with structured verification can significantly outperform single LLM; addresses hallucination problem
