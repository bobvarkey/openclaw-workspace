# Word of the Day — Sillage

**Word**: Sillage (si:'jaž)
**Meaning**: The scent that lingers in the air behind someone; a trail of perfume.
**Origin**: French (*suivre* — to follow)

## Summary

A French-origin word borrowed into English, primarily used in fragrance discussions to describe how a perfume projects and leaves a trail. Can be used metaphorically for any presence or impression left behind.
