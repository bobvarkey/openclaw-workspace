## Geniohyoid Muscle

### Anatomy
- **Location**: Floor of mouth, above mylohyoid
- **Origin**: Genial tubercles of mandible
- **Insertion**: Body of hyoid bone
- **Innervation**: C1 nerve fibers traveling with hypoglossal nerve (CN XII)
- **Function**: Elevates and pulls hyoid forward; depresses mandible (opens mouth)

### Clinical Relevance
- Part of suprahyoid muscle group
- Important for swallowing and airway protection
- Assessed in **hypoglossal nerve (CN XII)** evaluation
- Fasciculations in geniohyoid may indicate **bulbar ALS**
