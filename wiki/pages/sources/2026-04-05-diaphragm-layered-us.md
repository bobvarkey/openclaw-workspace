## Diaphragm Ultrasound Appearance

### Layered Structure
The diaphragm on ultrasound appears as a **three-layer structure**:

| Layer | Appearance | Structure |
|-------|------------|----------|
| **Hyperechoic (bright) border** | Superior | **Pleura** (costal diaphragmatic pleura) |
| **Hypoechoic (dark) center** | Middle | **Muscular portion** of diaphragm |
| **Hyperechoic (bright) border** | Inferior | **Peritoneum** |

### Zone of Apposition (ZOA)
- The diaphragm muscle attaches to the lower inner chest wall
- This creates the ZOA where diaphragmatic contractile force directs rib cage expansion
- The **hypoechoic muscular portion** is most relevant for:
  - Thickness measurement
  - Thickening fraction calculation
  - Function assessment

## Clinical Significance

### Diaphragm Function Assessment

| Measurement | Normal Value |
|------------|--------------|
| **Thickness** | Variable; compared to contralateral side |
| **Thickening fraction** | >30% during inspiration |
| **ZOA** | Evaluated at midaxillary line, 10th ICS |

### Depth Setting
- 2 cm depth setting suggests a focused assessment
- Adequate for visualizing the layered diaphragm structure
