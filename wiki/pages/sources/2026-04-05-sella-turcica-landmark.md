# Sella Turcica — Transnasal Anatomical Landmark

## Image Details

| Finding | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **View** | Lateral skull (fluoroscopic/radiographic) |
| **Landmark** | Sella turcica (pituitary fossa) |
| **Instrument** | Transnasal probe/catheter approaching |

## Sella Turcica

### Anatomy
- **Location**: Midline of the skull base
- **Structure**: Bony depression housing the pituitary gland
- **Landmark**: Important reference point for transnasal procedures

### Clinical Significance
- **Transnasal procedures**: Sella turcica is a key bony landmark
- **Approach path**: Instrument passes below and anterior to sella
- **Fluoroscopic guidance**: Used to confirm correct positioning during:
  - SPG block
  - Pituitary surgery
  - Endoscopic sinus surgery
  - Sphenoid sinus access

## Transnasal Approach Considerations

### Anatomical Relationships
| Structure | Relation to Sella |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Sphenoid sinus** | Anterior to sella |
| **SPG** | Posterior to middle turbinate, near lateral wall |
| **Carotid arteries** | Lateral to sella |
| **Optic chiasm** | Superior to sella |

### Safety
- Stay **anterior and lateral** to sella for SPG block
- Sella contains pituitary — avoid disruption
- Fluoroscopy confirms instrument position relative to landmarks
