# Abnormal Muscle Ultrasound Patterns

## Three Patterns of Abnormal Muscle Ultrasound

| Pattern | Appearance | Clinical Category | Example |
|---------|------------|-------------------|---------|
| **Pattern 1: Diffuse white** | Uniformly hyperechoic (bright) muscle | **Dystrophic** | Muscular dystrophies |
| **Pattern 2: Focally abnormal** | Localized areas of increased echogenicity | **Inflammatory** | Myositis, polymyositis |
| **Pattern 3: Moth-eaten** | Patchy, irregular hyperechoic and hypoechoic areas | **Neurogenic** | Motor neuron disease, radiculopathy |

---

## Pattern 1: Diffuse White

### Appearance
- **Uniformly hyperechoic (bright)** throughout the muscle
- Homogeneous increased echogenicity

### Pathophysiology
- Fat and fibrous tissue infiltration replacing normal muscle
- Characteristic of **muscular dystrophies**

### Examples
- Duchenne muscular dystrophy
- Becker muscular dystrophy
- Limb-girdle muscular dystrophies

---

## Pattern 2: Focally Abnormal

### Appearance
- **Localized areas** of increased echogenicity
- Surrounding muscle appears relatively normal

### Pathophysiology
- Focal inflammatory infiltrates
- Characteristic of **inflammatory myopathies**

### Examples
- Polymyositis
- Dermatomyositis
- Inclusion body myositis

---

## Pattern 3: Moth-Eaten

### Appearance
- **Patchy, irregular** pattern
- Mix of hyperechoic (bright) and hypoechoic (dark) areas
- "Moth-eaten" describes the irregular, eaten-away appearance

### Pathophysiology
- **Neurogenic atrophy** — affected fibers become bright while denervated fibers remain dark
- Characteristic of motor neuron disease and severe neurogenic conditions

### Examples
- ALS (amyotrophic lateral sclerosis)
- Cervical/lumbar radiculopathy
- Peripheral neuropathy

---

## Clinical Utility

| Pattern | Suggests | Next Step |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| Diffuse white | Dystrophic myopathy | Genetic testing, biopsy |
| Focally abnormal | Inflammatory myopathy | CK, autoimmune workup, biopsy |
| Moth-eaten | Neurogenic process | NCS/EMG, motor neuron disease workup |
