# Normal Diaphragm Thickening — Ultrasound

## Image Details

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Hemi-diaphragm** | Right |
| **Thickness at rest** | 0.30 cm (end expiration) |
| **Thickness at deep inspiration** | 0.70 cm |
| **Thickening fraction** | **130%** |

## Calculation

**Thickening Fraction** = (Inspiration − Rest) / Rest × 100

= (0.70 − 0.30) / 0.30 × 100

= 0.40 / 0.30 × 100

= **133% ≈ 130%**

## Interpretation

| Thickening Fraction | Interpretation |
|--------------------|---------------|
| **<30%** | Abnormal / impaired function |
| **30–50%** | Borderline / mild dysfunction |
| **>30%** | **Normal** (this case: 130%) |

This is **robust normal diaphragm function** — 130% thickening fraction indicates excellent contractile reserve.

## Normal Diaphragm Parameters

| Parameter | Normal Value |
|-----------|--------------|
| Thickness at rest | 0.2–0.3 cm |
| Thickness at inspiration | Increases proportionally |
| Thickening fraction | **>30%** |
| ZOA expansion | Visible |

## Clinical Context

### Why 130% is Robust
- Normal diaphragm typically shows 30–50% thickening fraction at tidal breathing
- 130% indicates **deep inspiration** — very good contractile reserve
- This right hemidiaphragm is functioning well

### Comparison
- Previous image showed left hemidiaphragm with **0% thickening fraction** (0.10 cm, atrophic/paralytic)
- This right hemidiaphragm at **130%** shows normal robust function
