# Rectus Abdominis Muscle Ultrasound

## Image Details

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Muscle** | Rectus abdominis |
| **Depth** | 6 cm |
| **Landmark** | 2 cm cranial to umbilicus |
| **Position** | Lateral over muscle convexity |
| **Annotation** | Calliper marks (+) for measurement |

## Rectus Abdominis Anatomy

### Location
- Anterior abdominal wall
- Runs vertically on either side of the midline
- From pubic symphysis/stub to xiphoid process/costal cartilages

### Structure
- Paired vertical muscles
- Separated by the **linea alba** (midline raphe)
- Covered by **rectus sheath** (aponeuroses of external/internal oblique, transversus abdominis)

### Clinical Relevance
- Assessment of generalized muscle changes
- Used in **sarcopenia assessment**
- Important in **body composition evaluation**
- Can show changes in neuromuscular diseases

## Ultrasound Technique

### Probe Position
- **Landmark**: 2 cm cranial to umbilicus
- **Orientation**: Lateral over muscle convexity
- **Depth setting**: 6 cm (allows visualization of entire muscle thickness)

### Measurement Points
- Muscle thickness (calipers)
- Echogenicity assessment (Heckmatt grading)
- Atrophy or hypertrophy evaluation

## Clinical Applications

| Indication | What to Assess |
|-----------|----------------|
| Sarcopenia | Muscle thickness, echogenicity |
| Neuromuscular disease | Diffuse changes, fasciculations |
| Body composition | Muscle mass estimation |
| Nutritional assessment | Muscle quality |
