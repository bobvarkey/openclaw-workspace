# AVM Assessment — What to Evaluate

## Core Assessment Parameters

### 1. Nidus
- **Size**
- **Compactness**
- **Eloquence** (adjacent functional brain areas)
- **Compartmentalisation** (multiple compartments)

### 2. Shunting
- Haemodynamics assessment
- Nidal vs fistulous type AVM
- ?Dural supply
- **Dynamic assessment required**

### 3. Perinidal Findings
- Perinidal angiogenesis
- Peri-intranidal aneurysm
- Perinidal arterial stenoses
- Flow aneurysms

### 4. Venous Drainage
- Number of venous outlets
- Relationship of outlets
- Deep vs superficial drainage
- Venous stenoses
- Venous ecstasias (ectasia)

### 5. Shared Venous Outflow
- Shared venous outflow between AVM and normal brain
- **Critical for treatment safety**

### 6. Effects on Surrounding Brain
- Edema
- Venous congestion
- Hydrocephalus
