# Nasopharyngeal Swab — Technique Guide

## Key Technique Instructions

| Instruction | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Direction** | Insert parallel to the zygoma |
| **Path** | Follow the superior border of the nasal turbinate |
| **Goal** | Along the floor of the nasal cavity toward the nasopharynx |

## Anatomical Landmarks

### Nasal Turbinate
- The swab path follows the **superior border** of the nasal turbinate
- The turbinate is a curved bony structure covered in mucosa in the lateral nasal wall

### Key Structures
- **Nasal turbinate**: Superior border guides swab placement
- **Nasal cavity floor**: The swab travels along this
- **Nasopharynx**: The target area for specimen collection

## Correct Swab Path

1. Insert swab **parallel to zygoma** (not upward toward the forehead)
2. Follow the **superior border** of the nasal turbinate
3. Advance along the **floor of the nasal cavity**
4. Continue until resistance met at the nasopharynx
5. Rotate and leave in place for several seconds
6. Withdraw and place in transport medium

## Common Errors

| Error | Problem |
|-------|---------|
| Swab angled upward | May hit nasal septum or cause trauma |
| Too shallow | Insufficient specimen |
| Too forceful | May cause bleeding or patient discomfort |

## Clinical Context

- Used for respiratory virus detection (influenza, COVID-19, RSV)
- Adequate specimen requires mucosal contact from the nasopharynx
- Proper technique improves test sensitivity
