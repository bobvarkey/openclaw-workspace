## Muscles Visualized

### m. digastricus (Digastric Muscle)
- **Anatomy**: Two bellies (anterior and posterior) united by an intermediate tendon
- **Origin**: Anterior belly from digastric fossa of mandible; posterior belly from mastoid notch
- **Insertion**: Via intermediate tendon to hyoid bone
- **Innervation**: Mandibular nerve (V3) for anterior belly; facial nerve (VII) for posterior belly
- **Function**: Elevates hyoid, depresses mandible, involved in swallowing

### m. geniohyoideus (Geniohyoid)
- **Anatomy**: Paired muscle above mylohyoid
- **Origin**: Genial tubercles of mandible
- **Insertion**: Hyoid body
- **Innervation**: C1 via hypoglossal nerve
- **Function**: Elevates hyoid, pulls it forward

### m. mylohyoideus (Mylohyoid)
- **Anatomy**: Forms the floor of the mouth
- **Origin**: Mylohyoid line of mandible
- **Insertion**: Hyoid body and mylohyoid raphe
- **Innervation**: Mandibular nerve (V3)
- **Function**: Elevates floor of mouth and hyoid

## Clinical Context

### Hypoglossal Nerve Assessment
These muscles are relevant for:
- **Hypoglossal nerve (CN XII)** function — tongue and suprahyoid muscles
- **Swallowing studies** — floor of mouth muscle coordination
- **Bulbar function assessment** — in ALS and other motor neuron diseases

### Neuromuscular Relevance
- Fasciculations in these muscles may indicate **bulbar-onset ALS**
- Atrophy suggests **denervation**
- Can be assessed via ultrasound alongside EMG

## Ultrasound Technique

### Probe Position
- **Transverse** under the chin
- Lateral to midline for optimal visualization
- Water or gel coupling

### Depth and Settings
- Lower depth settings (4–6 cm) typically sufficient
- Linear probe (high frequency)
