## Study 1: Hypoglossal Nerve Conduction — Novel Submental Electrode Placement

**Reference**: Oliveira AL et al. Muscle Nerve. 2026 Mar;73(3):427-433. PMID: 41416495

### Key Points
- Traditional hypoglossal NCS uses tongue electrodes → plagued by saliva interference
- Novel method: active electrode on submental region, reference contralaterally
- Study: 34 healthy adults

### Normative Data
- CMAP onset latency: mean 1.27 ms, upper limit 1.58 ms
- Amplitudes: >0.88 mV
- Decremental response: minimal (-0.53%)

### Clinical Significance
- Eliminates specialized tongue electrodes
- Maintains reproducibility
- Practical advantage for routine clinical use

## Study 2: Phrenic Nerve Conduction — Age- and Sex-Specific Normative Data

**Reference**: Castro J, de Carvalho M. J Clin Neurophysiol. 2026 Feb;43(2):176-182. PMID: 40554516

### Study Details
- Largest normative dataset for phrenic nerve NCS
- 132 healthy subjects aged 23–90 years

### Key Findings
- Age increases latency by ~0.25 ms/decade
- Men show higher amplitudes than women
- Left-sided CMAP amplitudes significantly larger

### Abnormal Thresholds
| | Men | Women |
|---|---|---|
| Latency | >9.5 ms | >8.5 ms |
| Amplitude | <0.62 mV | <0.48 mV |

### Clinical Significance
- Assessment of diaphragm compromise in neuromuscular diseases
- Important for ALS, Guillain-Barré, other neuromuscular disorders

## Study 3: AI Framework INSPIRE Enhances EMG/NCS Interpretation

**Reference**: Gorenshtein A et al. Clin Neurophysiol. 2025 Sep;177:2110792. PMID: 40540846

### System Design
- Multi-agent AI system called INSPIRE
- Integrates: patient history, complaints, EDX findings
- Three agents with tool access to reference tables and neuromuscular textbooks

### Performance
- 92.2% accuracy for normal vs abnormal detection
- vs 62.6% for base LLM
- p<0.001 (statistically significant)

### Key Innovation
- Addresses LLM hallucinations through structured verification
- Potential for standardizing and accelerating EDX reporting

Three studies covering:

Clinical takeaway: Nerve conduction studies continue to evolve with better normative data and AI assistance, improving diagnostic precision in neuromuscular disease.
