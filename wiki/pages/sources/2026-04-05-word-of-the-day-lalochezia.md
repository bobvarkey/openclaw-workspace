# Lalochezia

**Pronunciation**: /ˌlæləˈkiːziə/ (likely)

**Part of Speech**: Noun

**Definition**: The emotional relief gained from using abusive or profane language.

**Origin**: Greek *lalos* (babble, chatter) + *chezia* (defecation) — literally "shitting words."

---

## Usage

- "After the frustrating meeting, she vented her lalochezia by swearing profusely in the car."
- "Sometimes a good dose of lalochezia is the healthiest thing you can do."
- "He let out a stream of lalochezia that would make a sailor blush."

## Notes

- A clinical-sounding word for a very human impulse
- The relief comes from both the physical act of expressing anger AND the social taboo-breaking of profanity
- Related to the concept of catharsis in psychology
- The Greek etymology is charmingly visceral

- [[Catharsis]] — emotional release through expression
