# Blood Clot Risk & HRT

## Key Message

**Transdermal estradiol + micronized progesterone shows NO increased risk of VTE** — same as baseline. This is the HRT regimen used in most women today.

**Important distinction**: Oral preparations (especially Prempro/ CEE+MPA) DO increase VTE risk. The WHI study used oral Prempro — not representative of modern HRT.

## Clinical Implications

### Why the WHI Study Was Misleading
- WHI used **oral Prempro** (conjugated equine estrogen + medroxyprogesterone acetate)
- This combination **DOES** increase VTE risk
- Results were generalized to all HRT — incorrectly
- Modern HRT uses **transdermal estrogen + micronized progesterone** — different risk profile

### Modern HRT Recommendation
- **Transdermal estradiol**: Bypasses first-pass liver effect → no increased clotting risk
- **Micronized progesterone** (e.g., Prometrium): Better safety profile than synthetic progestins
- **Combined**: Does not increase VTE risk vs baseline

### Absolute Risk Context
Even oral HRT risk (37/10,000) is lower than pregnancy third trimester (114/10,000) — important to keep risk in perspective.
