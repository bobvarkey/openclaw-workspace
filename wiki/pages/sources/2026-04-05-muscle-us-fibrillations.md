# Dynamic Muscle Ultrasound — Detecting Fibrillations

**Source**: Editorial in Clinical Neurophysiology (Elsevier)
**URL**: www.elsevier.com/locate/clinph

## Key Finding

Dynamic muscle ultrasound can now detect **fibrillations** — previously only detectable by needle EMG.

## What This Means

Traditionally:
- **Fibrillations** = spontaneous muscle fiber activity seen on needle EMG
- Required invasive needle insertion
- Only detectable in muscles accessible to EMG needle

Now:
- **Dynamic muscle ultrasound** can visualize fibrillations without needles
- Non-invasive assessment of denervation
- Opens up muscles difficult to access with EMG

## Clinical Implications

### Advantages of Ultrasound Fibrillation Detection
- **Non-invasive** — no needle insertion
- **Visual** — can see where in the muscle fibrillations occur
- **Dynamic** — can observe in real-time during movement
- **Broader coverage** — muscles difficult to reach with EMG

### Significance
- Dynamic muscle ultrasound is "conquering another domain of needle EMG"
- May reduce need for some needle EMG procedures
- Better for patients who can't tolerate needle examination
- Useful for screening before proceeding to EMG

## Fibrillations on EMG vs Ultrasound

| Feature | Needle EMG | Muscle Ultrasound |
|---------|-----------|------------------|
| Invasiveness | Yes (needle) | No |
| Detection method | Electrical activity | Visual observation of muscle micro-movements |
| Coverage | Limited by needle placement | Wider field of view |
| Quantification | Amplitude, duration | Visual intensity |
