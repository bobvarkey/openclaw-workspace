# Status Migrainosus — Clinical Management Pathway

## Initial Assessment

- Known history with gradual onset
- No red flags
- Episode like previous presentations except for duration and intensity

---

## Initial Management (All Patients)

- **IV Fluids ++ (0.9% sodium chloride)**
- **Suitable environment**

---

## Treatment Pathways

### Pathway 1: Treatment Naïve
### Pathway 2: Pre NSAID Treated / NSAID Intolerant
### Pathway 3: Pre Triptan Treated

---

## Step 1: Dopamine Antagonists

- **IV prochlorperazine** OR **IV metoclopramide 10mg**
- Prochlorperazine may be more sedative
- Watch for: orthostatic hypotension, extrapyramidal side effects
- If intrusive side effects occur: give **IM/IV procyclidine 5mg**
- Wait 10 minutes; if not resolved, give further 5mg (may take 30 minutes to work)

---

## Step 2: Analgesics

### For Treatment Naïve & Pre Triptan Treated (NSAID preferred):
- **IV ketorolac 30mg** OR **IV acetylsalicylate 1g**
- ± **IV acetaminophen 1g**

### For Pre NSAID Treated / NSAID Intolerant:
- **IV acetaminophen 1g**

### Note for Pre Triptan Treated:
Consider if large volume emesis within 30 min of oral triptan OR >2 hrs post-oral sumatriptan

---

## Step 3: Triptans

- **SC sumatriptan 6mg**
- Criteria: Age >18 and <65 years
- No active unstable cardiovascular/cerebrovascular concerns
- No past history of vascular end-events

---

## Step 4: Magnesium (If Rx Failure)

- **IV magnesium sulfate 500mg–1g over 10–15 mins**
- Monitor BP (potential hypotension — risk reduced by prior fluid bolus)
- Monitor respiratory rate (potential respiratory depression)

---

## Step 5: Persistent Treatment Failure

### Options:
1. **IV chlorpromazine 5–10mg** — repeat 5mg every 30 min (max 25mg, 0.1mg/kg)
   - Higher extrapyramidal risk than prochlorperazine
   - Treat EPS with IM/IV procyclidine as above
   - Warn about sedation and dysphoria

2. **IV sodium valproate 500mg** — for men and post-menopausal women

3. **IV levetiracetam 500mg** — infusion protocol (as for status epilepticus, reduced dose)

4. **IV DHE 0.5–1mg** — followed by inpatient repetitive DHE per Raskin protocol

---

## Additional Intervention

- ± **Peripheral cranial nerve block: GON+ (Greater Occipital Nerve)**
