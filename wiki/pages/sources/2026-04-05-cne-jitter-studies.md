# CNE Jitter Studies — Interpretation of Results

**Source**: Clinical guideline document
**Reference**: Stahlberg et al, 2016

---

## Purpose

Concentric needle electrode (CNE) jitter studies are used to diagnose **neuromuscular junction disorders**, most notably **Myasthenia Gravis**. Jitter refers to the variability in the time between muscle fiber depolarizations.

---

## Criteria for Abnormal Study

- **Minimum acceptable potentials**: 20 acceptable potential pairs
- **Abnormal if either**:
  - Mean (or median) value exceeds upper limit reference
  - **Greater than 10%** of individual pairs are abnormal

---

## Caveats & Reporting

- **Exclusion**: Jitter values <5 µs should be excluded from analysis
- **Measurement preference**: For jitter >150 µs, **median values** are preferred over mean (more robust to outliers)
- **Additional data**: Always report **percentage of blocking**

---

## Reference Values for CNE Jitter Studies

| Muscle | Mean MCD Upper Limit (µs) | Individual Data Upper Limit (µs) |
|--------|:-------------------------:|:------------------------------:|
| Orbicularis oculi (vol) | 31 | 45 |
| Orbicularis oculi (stim) | 27 | 36 |
| Frontalis (vol) | 28 | 38 |
| Frontalis (stim) | 21 | 28 |
| Extensor digitorum (vol) | 30 | 43 |
| Extensor digitorum (stim) | 24 | 35 |

*All values in microseconds (µs). MCD = Mean Consecutive Difference.*

---

## Key Notes

- **Vol** = volitional activation (patient activates muscle voluntarily)
- **Stim** = stimulated activation (nerve is electrically stimulated)
- Stimulated studies generally yield lower jitter values (more controlled)
- Orbicularis oculi is the most commonly studied muscle for ocular MG

---

## Clinical Relevance

- Abnormal jitter indicates **neuromuscular junction dysfunction**
- Used for: Myasthenia Gravis diagnosis, Lambert-Eaton Myasthenic Syndrome,botulism
- Combined with clinical findings and anti-AChR/anti-MuSK antibodies for diagnosis
