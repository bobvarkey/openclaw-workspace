# Growlery

**Pronunciation**: /ˈɡraʊləri/

**Part of Speech**: Noun

**Definition**: A place of refuge or sanctuary for use while one is out of sorts or in an ill humor.

**Origin**: English; from the verb *growl* + the suffix *-ery* (denoting a place of activity or condition) — literally "a place for growling."

---

## Usage

- "After the argument, she retreated to her growlery to cool off."
- "Everyone needs a growlery — a little corner where you can be grumpy in peace."
- "He disappeared into the growlery for an hour after the stressful meeting."

## Notes

- Coined or popularized in the 19th century
- Evocative of the Victorian concept of having a private retreat for "taking the sulks"
- The suffix *-ery* creates a place noun (like *bakery*, *nursery*, *dairy*)
- A delightful word for a very specific human need: a place to be quietly miserable

## Related Words
- *Nook*: a small sheltered corner
- *Den*: a private room for relaxation
- *Retreat*, *sanctum*, *haven*
