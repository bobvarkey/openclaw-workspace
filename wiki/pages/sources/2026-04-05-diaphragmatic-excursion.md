# Diaphragmatic Excursion — M-Mode Ultrasound

## Image Details

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Mode** | B-mode and M-mode |
| **Device** | GE Venue |
| **Landmarks** | Liver, Diaphragm |
| **Measurement** | Diaphragmatic Excursion (DE) |

## B-Mode Image
- Shows anatomical view with **liver** as acoustic window
- **Diaphragm** visible as hyperechoic line
- Allows identification of diaphragm position relative to liver

## M-Mode Image
- **M-mode (Motion mode)** traces diaphragm movement over time
- **DE (Diaphragmatic Excursion)** = vertical distance the diaphragm moves during respiration
- Allows **quantitative measurement** of:
  - Tidal excursion (normal breathing)
  - Maximum excursion (deep breathing)
  - Respiratory rate

## Diaphragmatic Excursion Values

| Parameter | Normal Adult | Abnormal |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| **Quiet breathing** | 1.8–3.0 cm | <1.5 cm |
| **Deep breathing** | 5–7 cm | <3 cm |
| **Unilateral** | 2–4 cm | Asymmetric |

## Technical Guidelines

| Parameter | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Depth** | Adjust to optimally capture excursion |
| **Gain** | Optimize contrast with surrounding structures |
| **Focus** | Position M-line perpendicular to diaphragm movement |
| **Sweep speed** | Obtain at least 3 respiratory cycles within 1 frame |

## Clinical Significance

| Finding | Interpretation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Reduced DE** | Diaphragm weakness, paralysis, or phrenic nerve injury |
| **Asymmetric DE** | Unilateral phrenic nerve dysfunction |
| ** Paradoxical movement** | Complete diaphragm paralysis |
