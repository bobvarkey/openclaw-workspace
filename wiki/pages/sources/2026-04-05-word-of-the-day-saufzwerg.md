# Saufzwerg

**Pronunciation**: /ˈzaʊ̯ft͡svɛʁk/ (approximate)

**Part of Speech**: Noun

**Definition**: "Drinking dwarf" — a small person who, due to their size, tolerates very little alcohol and therefore gets drunk quickly.

**Origin**: German compound — *saufen* (to drink, colloquial/slang) + *Zwerg* (dwarf)

---

## Usage

- "He's a real Saufzwerg — two beers and he's dancing on tables."
- "I am, unfortunately, something of a Saufzwerg myself."
- "The party had a regular Saufzwerg who was tipsy after half a glass."

## Notes

- A charming example of German compound word construction
- The contrast between "dwarf" (often a mythic, powerful figure) and "drinking" (vulnerability from alcohol) creates dark humor
- Related to the concept of low alcohol tolerance due to body size/mass
- Similar concept exists in English as "cheap date" but with more evocative imagery
