# Fibromyalgia & Chronic Pain Management — Clinical Reference

## Pharmacological Treatments

### First-Line Oral Analgesics

| Drug | Dose Range | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **Paracetamol** | 4–6 g/day (qds) | Max 4g in elderly |
| **Pregabalin** | 300–600 mg/day | First-line for fibromyalgia |
| **Duloxetine** | 20–60 mg/day | SNRI; mood and pain |
| **Amitriptyline** | Low dose | TCA; neuropathic component |

### NSAIDs & Analgesics

| Drug | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Ibuprofen** | NSAIDs for inflammation component |
| **Flupirtine** | Non-opioid analgesic |
| **Ultracet** | Tramadol + paracetamol |
| **Tapentadol** | Opioid analgesic |
| **Dynapar AQ IV** | Diclofenac IV injection |
| **Pentanerve GRDS** | 300/450/600 mg dosing |

### Coxibs

- **Paloxicoxib**
- **Etoricoxib**

### Anticonvulsants (for neuropathic component)

- **Amitriptyline**
- **Pregabalin** 300–600 mg/day
- **Gabapentin**

## Advanced / Specialty Treatments

### Low-Dose Naltrexone (LDN)
- **Start**: 0.5 mg
- **Titration**: Weekly increases
- **Target**: 2–4.5 mg
- **Side effects**: Vivid dreams, headaches

### GLP-1 Agonists for Fibromyalgia
- Emerging indication

### Ketamine Infusion Therapy (KIT Protocol)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Dose** | 0.5 mg/kg ketamine |
| **Duration** | Infused over 40 minutes |
| **Schedule** | 5 consecutive days |

**Monitoring during KIT:**
- ECG, pulse oximetry, BP every 15 min
- Full resuscitation equipment available
- Ondansetron 8 mg IV for nausea prevention

**Adverse events requiring discontinuation/emergency response:**
- New ECG abnormalities (bradycardia, tachycardia, atrial fibrillation)
- Symptomatic hypertension or hypotension (>20% from baseline)
- Perceptual disturbances
- Hemodynamic changes
- Respiratory concerns

**Post-infusion:** Monitor 30 min, discharge with a known driver.

## Interventional / Physical Treatments

### TENS (Transcutaneous Electrical Nerve Stimulation)
- Combining TENS with physical therapy significantly reduces movement-evoked pain and fatigue in fibromyalgia

### Intranasal / Regional Blocks

#### Intranasal Lidocaine for SPG Block
- For migraine, cluster, post-dural puncture headache
- 5–10 minutes duration

#### Sphenopalatine Ganglion (SPG) Block
- **Approach**: Transnasal
- **Path**: Parallel to zygoma; follow superior border of middle turbinate
- **Endpoint**: Pterygopalatine fossa
- **Visualization**: Fluoroscopy confirms tip above middle turbinate
- **Typical fluid-fluid level** in pterygopalatine fossa
- **Schedule**: Weekly for 3 treatments, then maintenance every 6–8 weeks if satisfactory relief

## Combination Therapy Notes

- Many fibromyalgia patients, especially elderly with sciatica, are on **at least 2 medications**
- Common combinations:
  - Paracetamol qds (4–6 g range) + Pregabalin (300–600 range)
  - Paracetamol + Duloxetine (20–60 range)
