# Sillage

**Pronunciation**: /si:'jaž/

**Part of Speech**: Noun

**Definition**: The scent that lingers in the air behind someone; a trail of perfume.

**Origin**: French, from the verb *suivre* (to follow) — literally "the trail behind."

---

## Usage

- "She left the room and her sillage lingered for hours."
- "The perfume had a subtle sillage that wasn't overpowering."

## Notes

- Often used in fragrance discussions to describe how a perfume projects and trails behind the wearer
- Can also be used metaphorically for any presence or impression left behind
- Borrowed directly into English from French perfume culture
