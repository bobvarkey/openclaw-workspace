## Naltrexone — Mechanism

### Standard Dose Naltrexone
- **Opioid antagonist** used for opioid use disorder and alcohol use disorder
- Blocks opioid receptors (mu, kappa, delta)
- Standard dose: 50–100 mg/day

### Low Dose Naltrexone (LDN)
- **Low dose**: 0.5–4.5 mg (typically 1.5–4.5 mg)
- **Different mechanism** at low doses:
  - Transient opioid blockade → upregulated endogenous opioid production
  - Increased endorphin and enkephalin release
  - TLR4 (Toll-like receptor 4) modulation → anti-inflammatory effects

## Key Considerations

### Drug Interactions
- **Contraindicated with opioid medications** — can precipitate withdrawal
- Requires opioid-free period (7–14 days depending on opioid half-life)
- Caution with liver impairment

### Side Effects
- Vivid dreams (common)
- Sleep disturbances
- GI upset
- Headache

### LDN Disclaimer
- Not FDA-approved for most "off-label" uses
- Evidence is limited and mixed
- Should be prescribed by a qualified healthcare provider
