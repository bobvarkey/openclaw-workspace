# Diaphragm Thickness and DTF — Normal Values

## Diaphragm Thickness (Seated, Tidal Breathing, End-Expiration)

| Side | Male | Female |
|------|------|--------|
| **Right** | 2.1 ± 0.4 mm | 1.9 ± 0.4 mm |
| **Left** | 2.0 ± 0.4 mm | 1.7 ± 0.3 mm |

## Diaphragm Thickening Fraction (DTF) (Seated, Tidal Breathing, End-Expiration)

| Side | Male | Female |
|------|------|--------|
| **Right** | 32 ± 15% | 35 ± 16% |
| **Left** | 30 ± 14% | 33 ± 15% |

## DTF Formula

**DTF** = (Thickness at end-inspiration − Thickness at end-expiration) / Thickness at end-expiration × 100

## Clinical Thresholds

| DTF Value | Clinical Significance |
|-----------|---------------------|
| **DTF max <20%** | Diaphragm dysfunction |
| **DTF <25–33%** | Predicts **weaning failure** |
| **DTF <20%** | Predicts **NIV failure** |

## Clinical Applications

### Weaning from Mechanical Ventilation
- DTF <25–33% associated with **weaning failure**
- Diaphragm weakness is a major cause of prolonged mechanical ventilation
- DTF can guide timing of weaning trials

### Non-Invasive Ventilation (NIV)
- DTF <20% predicts **NIV failure**
- Patients with low DTF may require intubation
- Helps risk-stratify patients for NIV success

### Diaphragm Dysfunction
- DTF max <20% indicates **diaphragm dysfunction**
- Can be unilateral or bilateral
- Causes: phrenic nerve injury, ALS, ICU-acquired weakness
