# Vaccines Before Monoclonal Antibody Therapies

## General Principle

Vaccinations should ideally be completed **4–6 weeks before** initiating monoclonal antibody (mAb) therapy when possible.

## Live Vaccines (Caution with Immunosuppression)

| Vaccine | Timing Relative to mAb |
|--------|----------------------|
| **MMR** (Measles, Mumps, Rubella) | Live — avoid during/before mAb therapy if possible |

## Killed/Inactivated Vaccines (Safe)

| Vaccine | Type |
|---------|------|
| **Pneumococcal** (PPSV23, PCV13) | Killed polysaccharide/conjugate |
| **Influenza** | Killed/inactivated |
| **DPT** (Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus) | Killed toxoid/cellular |
| **Polio** (IPV — Inactivated) | Killed/inactivated |
| **RZV** (Shingrix — Recombinant Zoster Vaccine) | Recombinant (not live) |
| **Hepatitis B** | Recombinant subunit |

## Live Vaccines — Key Considerations

| Vaccine | Recommendation |
|--------|----------------|
| **MMR** | Avoid with immunosuppressive mAbs; give 4–6 weeks before if possible |
| **Varicella** | Live — similar caution |
| **Yellow Fever** | Live — avoid if immunosuppressed |

## Killed Vaccines — Safe to Give

Can generally be administered during mAb therapy:
- Pneumococcal
- Influenza (inactivated)
- Hepatitis B
- RZV (Shingrix — recombinant, not live)

## Clinical Notes

- Immunocompromised patients have altered vaccine responses
- Timing vaccines before immunosuppression is preferred
- Always check specific mAb mechanism and immunocompromise level
