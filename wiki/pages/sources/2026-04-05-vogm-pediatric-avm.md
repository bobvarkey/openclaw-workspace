# VOGM & Pediatric High-Flow Vascular Malformations

## Epidemiology

| Condition | Incidence |
|-----------|-----------|
| **VOGM** | 1 per 58,000 live births |
| **NGPAVF** (Non-Galenic Pial AVF) | 0.1 per 100,000 (even rarer) |

## Differential Diagnosis of High-Flow Vascular Malformations in Neonates

| Rank | Diagnosis |
|------|----------|
| 1 | **VOGM** — Vein of Galen Malformation |
| 2 | **NGPAVF** — Non-Galenic Pial AVF |
| 3 | **Dural AVF** |
| 4 | **AVM** — pial arteriovenous malformation |

## Key Distinction

| Feature | VOGM | NGPAVF |
|---------|------|--------|
| Location | Median prosencephalic vein | Other pial locations |
| Anatomy | Choroidal AV shunt into prosencephalic vein | Direct pial AV fistula |
| Rarity | 1:58,000 | 0.1:100,000 |

## Falcine Size

The **size of the falx cerebri** determines the **NAR** (Nidal Arterial Ratio?) in VOGM — falcine artery involvement correlates with anatomy.

## Intrauterine Treatment

- VOGM may be treated **intrauterine** (in utero)
- Emerging fetal therapy for selected cases
- Goal: prevent heart failure, preserve brain development
