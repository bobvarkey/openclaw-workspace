# Heckmatt Muscle Echogenicity Grading Scale

## Overview

The Heckmatt scale is a standardized grading system for assessing muscle echogenicity on ultrasound. It correlates with histopathological changes in muscle disease.

## Grades

| Grade | Description | Clinical Interpretation |
|-------|-------------|------------------------|
| **1** | Normal | Healthy muscle |
| **2** | Slight hyperechogenicity | Mild changes; early disease or normal variant |
| **3** | Texture disruption, some attenuation | Moderate changes; consistent with myopathy or neurogenic change |
| **4** | Texture unrecognizable, strong attenuation | Severe changes; advanced myopathy or chronic neurogenic change |

## Clinical Use

### Indications
- Suspected myopathy (inflammatory, metabolic, muscular dystrophies)
- Neurogenic disorders (assessing muscle changes from denervation)
- Monitoring disease progression or treatment response
- Screening tool before proceeding to EMG or biopsy

### Interpretation

**Higher echogenicity** (brighter muscle on ultrasound) indicates:
- Increased fat/fibrosis within muscle
- Muscle fiber atrophy or loss
- Inflammatory infiltrates

**Lower echogenicity** (darker muscle) = healthier muscle with normal architecture

## Correlation with EMG and Biopsy

| Heckmatt Grade | Typical EMG | Biopsy Findings |
|----------------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Normal | Normal |
| 2 | May be normal or mild changes | Mild fiber size variation |
| 3 | Myopathic or neurogenic changes | Significant pathology |
| 4 | Severe myopathic changes | End-stage changes |

## Limitations

- Operator-dependent (requires experienced sonographer)
- Age-related changes in echogenicity
- Variable thresholds between equipment
- Best used alongside clinical assessment, EMG, and CK levels
