# Low Flow Cerebral Vascular Malformations

## Types

| Type | Characteristic |
|------|---------------|
| **Capillary telangiectasia** | Small capillary dilations; often incidental |
| **Venous angioma (DVA)** | Developmental venous anomaly; benign |
| **Cavernoma (Cavernous malformation)** | Mulberry-like vascular spaces; cavernous hemangioma |

## Capillary Telangiectasia

- Small, dilated capillaries
- Usually asymptomatic
- Often found incidentally on MRI
- Common in brainstem

## Venous Angioma (DVA — Developmental Venous Anomaly)

- Collector veins in "medusa head" pattern
- Most common cerebral vascular malformation
- Usually benign
- May be associated with cavernous malformation

## Cavernoma

- Mulberry-like appearance
- Low-flow vascular spaces
- Can cause seizures, hemorrhage (rare)
- "Popcorn" appearance on MRI
