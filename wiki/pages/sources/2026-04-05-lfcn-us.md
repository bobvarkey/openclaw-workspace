# Lateral Femoral Cutaneous Nerve (LFCN) Ultrasound

## Lateral Femoral Cutaneous Nerve (LFCN)

### Anatomy
- **Origin**: L2-L3 nerve roots
- **Course**: Emerges at lateral border of psoas, crosses iliacus, passes under inguinal ligament near ASIS
- **Termination**: Supplies skin of lateral thigh

### Clinical Significance
- **Meralgia paresthetica**: Compression/entrapment of LFCN
- Symptoms: Burning, numbness, tingling on lateral thigh
- Common causes: Tight clothing, obesity, pregnancy, belt/straps

## Ultrasound Technique

### Probe Position
- Anterior hip, distal to inguinal ligament
- Lateral to sartorius, medial to TFL

### Key Landmarks
- **ASIS** (Anterior Superior Iliac Spine)
- **Sartorius**: Medial to LFCN
- **TFL**: Lateral to LFCN
- **LFCN**: Visible as hypoechoic structure between these muscles

## Clinical Notes from Source

### Adductor Evaluation
> "Pubic symphysis more medially. The adductor musculature can then be evaluated by moving the probe distally. Three adductor muscles are found from anterior to posterior with the adductor longus, brevis and magnus. The common adductor tendon is usually split by this point, the larger anterior component being the tendon of the adductor longus. The gracilis overlies the adductors and can be evaluated here."

### Adductor Muscles (Anterior to Posterior)
1. **Adductor longus**
2. **Adductor brevis**
3. **Adductor magnus**

### Additional Structure
- **Gracilis**: Overlies the adductors
