# Diaphragm Atrophy — Ultrasound Finding

## Image Details

| Finding | Value |
|---------|-------|
| **Hemi-diaphragm** | Left |
| **Thickness at rest** | 0.10 cm (severely atrophic) |
| **Thickening with inspiration** | None |
| **Thickening fraction** | 0% |

## Clinical Significance

### Diaphragm Atrophy
- **Thickness at rest <0.15 cm** = severely atrophic diaphragm
- Normal diaphragm thickness at rest: approximately 0.2–0.3 cm
- 0.10 cm = severely reduced (approximately 50% of normal)

### Thickening Fraction = 0%
- No thickening with inspiration
- Indicates **diaphragm paralysis**
- The diaphragm is not contracting functionally

## Diaphragm Function Assessment

### Normal Values

| Parameter | Normal |
|-----------|--------|
| Thickness at rest | 0.2–0.3 cm |
| Thickening fraction | >30% |
| ZOA expansion | Visible with inspiration |

### Abnormal Findings

| Finding | Interpretation |
|---------|---------------|
| Thickness <0.15 cm | Severe atrophy |
| Thickening fraction 0% | Paralysis/no contraction |
| No ZOA movement | Phrenic nerve dysfunction |

## Causes of Diaphragm Atrophy/Paralysis

| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Phrenic nerve injury** | Nerve damage → no innervation |
| **ALS** | Motor neuron death → diaphragm denervation |
| **Guillain-Barré syndrome** | Demyelination of phrenic nerve |
| **Surgery** | Direct phrenic nerve injury |
| **Radiation** | Phrenic nerve damage |

## Clinical Context

This finding (left hemidiaphragm atrophy, 0% thickening) is consistent with:
- **Diaphragm paralysis** from phrenic nerve injury
- **Chronic denervation** from ALS or other motor neuron disease
- **Severe neuromuscular junction dysfunction**
