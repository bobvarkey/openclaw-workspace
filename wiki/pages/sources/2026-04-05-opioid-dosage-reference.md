# Opioid Analgesic Dosage Reference

## Opioid Dose Equivalence & Limits

| Opioid | Initial Dose | Max Daily Dose | Frequency |
|--------|-------------|----------------|-----------|
| **Morphine** | 5–10 mg | 60 mg | — |
| **Codeine** | 15–60 mg | 240 mg | q6h |
| **Oxycodone** | 10 mg/24h | 400 mg/24h | With increments |
| **Tramadol** | 50 mg | 400 mg | qds (QDS) |

## Individual Drug Details

### Morphine
- **Initial**: 5–10 mg
- **Maximum**: 60 mg/24h
- **Notes**: Standard opioid for moderate-severe pain

### Codeine
- **Dose**: 15–60 mg
- **Max**: 240 mg/24h
- **Frequency**: Every 6 hours
- **Notes**: Pro-drug; requires CYP2D6 for activation

### Oxycodone
- **Initial**: 10 mg/24h
- **Max**: 400 mg/24h
- **Notes**: Titrate in increments; extended-release formulation

### Tramadol
- **Dose**: 50 mg
- **Frequency**: QDS (four times daily)
- **Max**: 400 mg/24h
- **Notes**: Weak opioid + SNRI properties; CYP2D6 dependent

## Conversion Notes

| From | To | Approximate Conversion |
|------|----|----------------------|
| Oral morphine | Oral oxycodone | 2:1 |
| IV morphine | Oral morphine | 1:3 |

## Cautions

| Drug | Key Caution |
|------|------------|
| **All opioids** | Respiratory depression, sedation |
| **Codeine** | CYP2D6 variability (ultra-rapid metabolizers) |
| **Tramadol** | Serotonin syndrome risk; seizure risk |
| **Oxycodone** | Abuse potential; constipation |
