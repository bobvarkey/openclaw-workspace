# Why Are We Not Preventing Osteoporosis?

## Estradiol Dosages and Bone Benefits

### Oral Estradiol

| Dose | Bone Benefit |
|------|-------------|
| 0.5 mg oral estradiol | Increased femoral neck BMD 3.8% over 2 years; prevented spine bone loss |
| 1 mg oral estradiol | Increased lumbar spine BMD 5.2% over 2 years |
| 2 mg oral estradiol | Increased lumbar spine BMD 6.7% over 2 years |

### Transdermal Estradiol

| Dose | Bone Benefit |
|------|-------------|
| 0.025 mg | Increased lumbar spine BMD 2.37% over 2 years |
| 0.05 mg | Increased lumbar spine BMD 4.09% over 2 years |
| 0.1 mg | Increased lumbar spine BMD 4.70% over 2 years |

## Key Clinical Points

### Peak Bone Loss Timing
- **Peak bone loss occurs during perimenopause**
- Specifically: **1–2 years before the final menstrual period** to **2 years after**

### Critical Window for Intervention
- Bone loss **accelerates during late perimenopause**
- This represents a **critical opportunity to protect and preserve bone density**
- Estrogen therapy during this window can prevent significant bone loss

### Mechanism
- **Estrogen prevents bone loss**
- Estrogen deficiency → increased osteoclast activity → accelerated bone resorption
- Replacing estrogen maintains bone density

---

## Clinical Implications

- Women in perimenopause should be assessed for osteoporosis risk
- Late perimenopause (1–2 years pre/post FMP) is the optimal intervention window
- Both oral and transdermal estradiol preserve bone density
- Higher doses of oral estradiol show greater BMD gains (dose-response relationship)
