# Choosing the Needle — Single Fibre vs Concentric

## Key Points

- Concentric needle is **increasingly preferred** over single fibre needle
- **Advantages of CNE**: Low cost, disposable, "apparent single fibre peaks"
- **Caveat**: Jitter measured with CNE is less than SF measurements (values not interchangeable)

---

## Comparison Table: Single Fibre vs Concentric Needle

| Parameter | Single Fibre Needle | Concentric Needle |
|-----------|:------------------:|:-----------------:|
| LFF | 500 Hz | 1 kHz |
| Sweep | 0.5 msec/div | 1 msec/div |
| Gain | 200 µV | 50 µV |
| Amplitude | 200 µV | ≥50 µV |
| Rise time | <300 µsec | <300 µsec |

---

## Smallest Concentric Needle Specifications

- **Dimensions**: 25 mm × 0.3 mm (30 G)
- Common product: Dantec DCN Disposable Concentric Needle Electrodes

---

## Practical Notes

- **CNE jitter values are lower** than SFEMG values — reference values differ
- Concentric needles are **disposable** (infection control advantage)
- Lower cost makes CNE more accessible
- The "apparent single fibre peaks" with CNE require experience to interpret
- Both methods share the same rise time criterion (<300 µsec)

## Clinical Relevance

This comparison is important when:
- Setting up a jitter studies service
