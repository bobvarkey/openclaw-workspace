## Treatment Recommendations

### First-Line
- **CBT-I** (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia): Multi-component — sleep restriction, stimulus control, relaxation, sleep hygiene, cognitive restructuring. Most effective and durable treatment.
- **BBT-I** (Brief Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia): Abbreviated CBT-I focusing on sleep restriction and stimulus control. Good when full CBT-I unavailable.

### Second-Line / Adjunct
- Short-term pharmacotherapy (<3 months) if CBT-I unavailable
- Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR)
- Regular exercise (especially muscle endurance + walking in older adults)

### NOT Recommended
- Antipsychotic drugs (weak evidence, significant side effects)
- Benzodiazepines (dependence, falls, cognitive impairment)
- Diphenhydramine (no evidence, anticholinergic effects)
- Trazodone (no clinically significant benefit)
- Sleep hygiene education alone (not effective as monotherapy)
