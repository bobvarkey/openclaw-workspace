## Anatomy Visualized

### ASIS — Anterior Superior Iliac Spine
- Bony landmark of the pelvis
- Key reference point for hip/inguinal region ultrasound
- Palpable bony prominence at the upper edge of the hip bone

### TFL — Tensor Fasciae Latae
- **Location**: Lateral to the ASIS
- **Origin**: Anterior aspect of ASIS and anterior iliac crest
- **Insertion**: Iliotibial band (ITB)
- **Innervation**: Superior gluteal nerve (L4-S1)
- **Function**: Hip flexion, abduction, internal rotation

### Sartorius Muscle
- **Location**: Medial to the ASIS
- **Origin**: ASIS
- **Insertion**: Superior aspect of medial tibial condyle (pes anserinus)
- **Innervation**: Femoral nerve (L2-L4)
- **Function**: Hip flexion, abduction, external rotation; knee flexion

## Probe Position

- **Transverse** placement on anterior-lateral hip
- ASIS as the key bony landmark
- TFL lateral to ASIS, Sartorius medial to ASIS

## Clinical Context

### Hip Muscle Assessment
- TFL and Sartorius are key muscles around the hip
- Important for:
  - **Hip abduction assessment** (TFL)
  - **Sartorius assessment** (part of quadriceps group)
  - **Neuromuscular disease** affecting proximal muscles
  - **Sarcopenia assessment** (proximal muscle mass)

### Relevance to Muscle Ultrasound
- Can assess for:
  - Muscle thickness
  - Echogenicity (Heckmatt grading)
  - Fasciculations
  - Atrophy
