# Muscle Ultrasound — Comprehensive Clinical Reference

## Muscle Ultrasound Parameters & Findings

### Thickness
- Measurement of muscle depth at specific anatomical landmarks
- Used for baseline and longitudinal comparison

### Heckmatt Grading
| Grade | Finding |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Normal |
| 2 | Slight hyperechogenicity |
| 3 | Texture disruption, some attenuation |
| 4 | Texture unrecognizable, strong attenuation |

### Fasciculations
- Visible involuntary muscle twitches
- Sign of denervation (like fibrillations and positive sharp waves)
- **Pathologic fasciculations**: more regular activation frequency, increased amplitude, polyphasia, duration
- **Complex/unstable fasciculations**: peaks appearing/disappearing with sustained observation
- **Yoked discharges >10ms after initial discharge**: suspicious for pathology
- **Benign vs pathologic**: No definitive distinction, but pathologic tends to have MUP morphology (increased amplitude, polyphasia, duration)

### Fibrillations at High Frame Rate (≥20 fps)
- Dynamic muscle ultrasound at 20 Hz frame rate can detect fibrillations
- Previously only detectable by needle EMG
- Key breakthrough in non-invasive diagnostics

### Contraction Fascics (CFs)
- **Only appear with voluntary muscle contraction**
- **Disappear upon relaxation**
- Caused by visible contraction of **enlarged motor units** from reinnervation in long-standing neurogenic conditions

### Attenuation Patterns
| Pattern | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Diffuse | Throughout the muscle |
| Focal | Localized area |
| Moth-eaten | Scattered hyper-attenuation pattern |

- **Spasticity may change muscle attenuation**

## Key EMG/Ultrasound Correlation

### Signs of Chronic Neurogenic Changes
- Large, long duration, unstable polyphasic motor unit potentials
- Reduced recruitment
- Increased amplitude MUPs
- Increased duration MUPs
- Polyphasia

### Awaji Modified Criteria for ALS
Complex/unstable fasciculations = equal weight to fibrillations and positive sharp waves as denervation signs in chronic neurogenic context.

## Specific Muscles Assessed

### Rectus Abdominis
- Commonly assessed for generalized muscle changes

### Digastric and Geniohyoid
- Submental/submandibular region muscles
- Used in hypoglossal nerve assessment context

### Sartorius
- Commonly sampled muscle for quadriceps evaluation

### Diaphragm — Zone of Apposition (ZOA)
- **Crucial area** where diaphragm attaches to lower inner chest wall
- Creates stable base for efficient breathing and core stability
- Where diaphragm's contractile force directs rib cage expansion
- Generates intra-abdominal pressure
- Essential for: deep breaths, proper posture, balanced movement

**Diaphragm Assessment Landmarks:**
| Location | Finding |
|----------|---------|
| Midaxillary line, 10th ICS | Elevation ~30° |
| Midaxillary line | Fractional thickening >30% |
| Midclavicular line | Tidal volume breathing: 2 ± 0.5 cm; sniff volume |

## Sarcopenia & Body Composition Assessment

### Quadriceps Muscle Ultrasound
- **Subcutaneous fat measurement**
- **Quadriceps muscle thickness**
- Used for sarcopenia assessment

### Spleen Ultrasound (Body Composition Indicator)
| Finding | Measurement |
|---------|-------------|
| Normal spleen size | ≤12 cm |
| Splenomegaly ratio | Spleen:kidney length >1.25 |
| Shape | Rounder than oval |

**Causes of Large Kidney on US:**
- Diabetes mellitus
- Amyloidosis
- Lymphoma
- HIV

## Clinical Applications Summary

| Application | Key Findings |
|------------|-------------|
| Denervation | Fibs, PSWs, fasciculations |
| Chronic reinnervation | Large polyphasic MUPs, reduced recruitment |
| Myopathy | Heckmatt grade, atrophy patterns |
| Diaphragm function | ZOA, thickening fraction |
| Sarcopenia | Muscle thickness, subcutaneous fat |
| Systemic disease | Splenomegaly, organomegaly |
