# Diaphragm Ultrasound

## Zone of Apposition (ZOA)

The **Zone of Apposition** is the crucial anatomical area where the diaphragm muscle attaches to the lower inner chest wall.

### ZOA Function
- Creates stable base for efficient breathing and core stability
- Where diaphragm's contractile force directs rib cage expansion
- Generates intra-abdominal pressure
- Essential for:
  - Deep breaths
  - Proper posture
  - Balanced movement

## Assessment Landmarks

### Midaxillary Line — 10th Intercostal Space
- **Elevation angle**: ~30°
- **Fractional thickening**: >30% indicates normal function

### Midclavicular Line
- **Tidal volume breathing**: 2 ± 0.5 cm
- **Sniff volume**: Separate assessment

## Diaphragm Thickness

- Assessed at end-inspiration and end-expiration
- **Thickening fraction** = (End-inspiration − End-expiration) / End-expiration × 100
- Normal: >30% thickening fraction

## Clinical Applications

- **Phrenic nerve assessment** (diaphragm weakness = phrenic neuropathy)
- **Neuromuscular disease monitoring** (ALS, Guillain-Barré)
- **Respiratory failure workup**
- **Pre-operative respiratory assessment**
