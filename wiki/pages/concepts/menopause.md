# Menopause

## Definition

Menopause is the permanent cessation of menstruation due to loss of ovarian follicular activity. Defined clinically as **12 months of amenorrhea** in the absence of other pathological or physiological cause.

**Natural menopause**: Occurs between ages 45–55 (average ~51)

## Clinical Diagnosis

For women aged 45–55:
- **12 months amenorrhea** + **vasomotor symptoms** = **Clinical diagnosis**
- **No laboratory testing required**

## Vasomotor Symptoms

- Hot flashes (sudden warmth, flushing, sweating)
- Night sweats
- Affects ~75% of women
- Duration: median 7 years, can persist >10 years

## Stages

| Stage | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| **Perimenopause** | Menstrual irregularity begins; FSH fluctuates; symptoms may start |
| **Menopause** | 12 months amenorrhea; FSH >40 IU/L |
| **Postmenopause** | After 12 months amenorrhea; lower E2, higher FSH |

## Laboratory Diagnosis (When Needed)

| Marker | Value |
|--------|-------|
| FSH | >40 IU/L |
| Estradiol | <20–30 pg/mL |

**Note**: Perimenopausal FSH fluctuates — single measurement unreliable

## Treatment

- [[HRT]] — first-line for moderate-severe symptoms
- Non-hormonal options: SSRIs, gabapentin, lifestyle modification
- [[HRT Preparations]] — transdermal E2 + micronized P4 preferred
