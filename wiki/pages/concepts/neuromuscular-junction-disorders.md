# Neuromuscular Junction (NMJ) Disorders

## Definition

Disorders affecting the neuromuscular junction — the synapse between motor neuron and skeletal muscle.

## Major NMJ Disorders

| Disorder | Mechanism | Key Features |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| **Myasthenia Gravis (MG)** | Anti-AChR/MuSK antibodies | Fatigable weakness, ocular onset common |
| **Lambert-Eaton Myasthenic Syndrome (LEMS)** | Anti-VGCC antibodies (presynaptic) | Proximal weakness, autonomic symptoms, improved with use |
| **Botulism** | Clostridial toxin blocks ACh release | Descending paralysis, bulbar symptoms |
| **Congenital Myasthenic Syndromes** | Genetic defects in NMJ proteins | Childhood onset, stable course |

## Diagnostic Testing

### Jitter Studies (Most Sensitive)
- SFEMG/CNE jitter is the **most sensitive test** for NMJ disorders
- Abnormal even when routine EMG/NCS is normal
- Shows increased jitter and blocking

### Repetitive Nerve Stimulation (RNS)
- **MG**: >10% decrement at 3 Hz stimulation
- **LEMS**: Increment >100% after brief exercise or high-frequency stimulation

### Antibody Testing
- Anti-AChR, anti-MuSK (MG)
- Anti-VGCC, anti-amphiphysin (LEMS)

## Clinical Presentation Differences

| Feature | MG | LEMS |
|---------|-----|------|
| Weakness pattern | Ocular/bulbar first | Proximal limbs first |
| Fatigability | Worsens with use | Improves with use |
| Reflexes | Normal | Diminished |
| Autonomic symptoms | Rare | Common |
