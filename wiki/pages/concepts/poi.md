# POI (Premature Ovarian Insufficiency)

## Definition

Loss of normal ovarian function **before age 40**. Previously called "premature menopause" — but POI is preferred as ovarian function may be intermittent.

## Diagnostic Criteria

- **FSH >40 IU/L** on **two occasions, 4–6 weeks apart**
- Age <40 years
- 12 months amenorrhea or oligomenorrhea

## Key Differences from Natural Menopause

| Feature | POI | Natural Menopause |
|---------|-----|------------------|
| Age | <40 | >45 (typically 45–55) |
| FSH | >40 IU/L (confirmed x2) | >40 IU/L |
| Ovarian function | May be intermittent | Permanently ceased |
| Fertility | May retain some | Essentially none |
| Cause | Often idiopathic/autoimmune | Normal ovarian aging |

## Why Confirm with Repeat FSH?

FSH fluctuates significantly in POI. Two elevated readings 4–6 weeks apart confirm persistent ovarian dysfunction rather than a single aberrant value.

## Etiology

- **Idiopathic** (most common)
- Autoimmune (adrenal antibodies, thyroid antibodies)
- Chromosomal (Turner syndrome, Fragile X permutation)
- Iatrogenic (chemotherapy, radiation, bilateral oophorectomy)
- Infections (mumps, HIV, TB)

## Clinical Importance

- **Fertility implications** — may still ovulate intermittently
- **Long-term health risks** — osteoporosis, cardiovascular disease, cognitive decline (earlier than natural menopause)
- **HRT recommended** until at least age of natural menopause (~51)
