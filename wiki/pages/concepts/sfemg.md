# SFEMG (Single Fibre Electromyography)

## Definition

SFEMG is the gold standard technique for measuring jitter at the neuromuscular junction. It records electrical activity from individual muscle fibres using a specialized single fibre needle.

## Comparison with CNE

| Feature | SFEMG | CNE |
|---------|:-----:|:---:|
| Recording | Single muscle fibre | Small fibre population |
| LFF | 500 Hz | 1 kHz |
| Sweep | 0.5 ms/div | 1 ms/div |
| Gain | 200 µV | 50 µV |
| Amplitude | 200 µV | ≥50 µV |
| Cost | Higher | Lower |
| Disposability | Usually reusable | Disposable |
| Jitter values | Higher (true SF values) | Lower than true SF values |

**Important**: CNE jitter values are systematically lower than SFEMG values. Reference values are **not interchangeable** between the two methods.

## Clinical Use

SFEMG jitter studies are used to diagnose:
- [[Myasthenia Gravis]]
- [[Lambert-Eaton Myasthenic Syndrome]]
- [[Neuromuscular Junction Disorders]]

## Rise Time Criterion

Both SFEMG and CNE require:
- **Rise time <300 µsec** (ensures recording is from the same fibre)
