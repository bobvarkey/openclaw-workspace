# CBT-I (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia)

## Definition

The gold-standard, first-line treatment for chronic insomnia disorder. A multi-component behavioral therapy that addresses the underlying mechanisms of insomnia rather than just symptoms.

## Components

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Sleep Restriction** | Limiting time in bed to actual sleep time → increases sleep drive |
| **Stimulus Control** | Re-associating bed with sleep; no screen use, no clock-watching |
| **Relaxation Training** | Progressive muscle relaxation, breathing exercises |
| **Sleep Hygiene** | Caffeine timing, environment, exercise timing |
| **Cognitive Restructuring** | Challenging maladaptive beliefs about sleep |

## Evidence

- Most effective and durable treatment for chronic insomnia
- Recommended by AAFP 2024, VA/DOD 2025, AASM 2021
- More effective long-term than pharmacotherapy
- Effects persist after treatment ends (unlike medication)

## BBT-I (Brief Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia)

Abbreviated version focusing on sleep restriction and stimulus control. Good option when full CBT-I is unavailable.

## When to Refer

- Complex presentations
- Comorbid mental health conditions
- Treatment-resistant cases
- Suspected sleep disorders (OSA, narcolepsy)
