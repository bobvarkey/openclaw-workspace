# Dynamic Muscle Ultrasound

## Definition

Real-time muscle ultrasound that assesses muscle during voluntary contraction and relaxation, allowing visualization of muscle fiber behavior including spontaneous activity like fibrillations.

## Advances in Dynamic Muscle Ultrasound

### Key Development
Dynamic muscle ultrasound can now detect **fibrillations** — previously only detectable by needle EMG.

### Significance
- "Conquering another domain of needle EMG"
- Non-invasive alternative for detecting denervation
- Published in Clinical Neurophysiology (Elsevier editorial)

## What It Detects

| Finding | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Fibrillations** | Spontaneous muscle fiber contractions visible as micro-movements |
| **Fasciculations** | Gross involuntary muscle twitches |
| **Contractures** | Sustained muscle shortening |
| **Muscle thickness changes** | During contraction/relaxation |

## Advantages Over Traditional EMG

| Feature | Dynamic Muscle US | Needle EMG |
|---------|:----------------:|:----------:|
| Invasiveness | Non-invasive | Invasive (needle) |
| Field of view | Wide | Limited to needle tip |
| Visualization | Visual | Electrical only |
| Patient tolerance | High | Moderate |
| Real-time dynamics | Yes | Limited |

## Clinical Applications

- Detecting fibrillations without needles
- Screening muscles difficult to access with EMG
- Patients who can't tolerate needles
- Longitudinal monitoring of neuromuscular disease
