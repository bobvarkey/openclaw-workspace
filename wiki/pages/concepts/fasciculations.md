# Fasciculations

## Definition

Fasciculations are **visible involuntary twitches of muscle fibers** visible on muscle ultrasound or felt by the patient. They represent spontaneous discharge of a motor unit.

## Contraction Fascics (CFs)

- **Only appear with voluntary muscle contraction**
- **Disappear upon relaxation**
- Caused by **enlarged motor units** from muscle **reinnervation** in long-standing neurogenic conditions
- Visible on dynamic muscle ultrasound

## Pathologic vs Benign Fasciculations

**No definitive way to distinguish** benign from pathologic, but:

| Feature | Pathologic | Benign |
|---------|------------|--------|
| Frequency | More regular activation | Variable |
| Morphology | Increased amplitude, polyphasia, duration | Normal |
| Stability | Complex/unstable, peaks appearing/disappearing | Stable |
| Yoked discharges | >10ms after initial discharge = suspicious | Absent |

## Clinical Significance

### In Chronic Neurogenic Conditions
Complex/unstable fasciculations = **equal weight to fibrillations and positive sharp waves** as signs of denervation.

### Awaji Modified Criteria for ALS
Fasciculations are included as a **denervation sign** when:
- Complex or unstable morphology
- Associated with chronic neurogenic changes (large, polyphasic MUPs)
- Yoked discharges >10ms after initial discharge

## Signs of Chronic Reinnervation (with fasciculations)
- Voluntary MUPs with increased amplitude
- Increased duration
- Polyphasia
- Decreased recruitment (if concomitant UMN dysfunction, may not be clear)
