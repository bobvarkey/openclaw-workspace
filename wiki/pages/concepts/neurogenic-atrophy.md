# Neurogenic Atrophy

## Definition

Muscle wasting and structural changes that occur as a result of **denervation** — loss of nerve supply to muscle fibers. The "moth-eaten" pattern on ultrasound reflects this process.

## Pathophysiology

When a motor neuron dies (as in ALS):
- Its muscle fibers become **denervated**
- Surviving motor neurons sprout collaterals and **reinnervate** some fibers
- Mixed population: reinnervated (large) + denervated (small) fibers
- This creates the **patchy/moth-eaten** ultrasound appearance

## Ultrasound Pattern: Moth-Eaten

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Appearance | Patchy, irregular hyperechoic (bright) and hypoechoic (dark) areas |
| Meaning | Mix of reinnervated (bright) and atrophic (dark) fibers |
| Category | Neurogenic |

## EMG Findings in Neurogenic Atrophy

- **Increased amplitude** MUPs (reinnervated motor units)
- **Increased duration** MUPs
- **Polyphasia**
- **Reduced recruitment**
- **Fibrillations and positive sharp waves** (denervation signs)
- **Fasciculations** (especially in ALS)

## Clinical Conditions

| Condition | Mechanism |
|-----------|-----------|
| ALS | Motor neuron death → widespread denervation |
| Cervical/lumbar radiculopathy | Nerve root compression → segmental denervation |
| Peripheral neuropathy | Distal denervation |
| Post-polio syndrome | Motor neuron loss → late denervation |

## Muscle Ultrasound Patterns

| Pattern | Pathology | Examples |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Diffuse white** | Dystrophic | Muscular dystrophies |
| **Focally abnormal** | Inflammatory | Myositis |
| **Moth-eaten** | Neurogenic | ALS, radiculopathy |
