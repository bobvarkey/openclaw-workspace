# Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA)

## Definition

Sleep-disordered breathing characterized by repeated upper airway collapse during sleep, causing intermittent hypoxia and sleep fragmentation.

## STOP Questionnaire (Screening)

| Item | Stands For |
|------|------------|
| S | Snoring |
| T | Tiredness (daytime sleepiness) |
| O | Observed apneas |
| P | Pressure (hypertension) |

**Scoring**: ≥2 positive = high risk → recommend polysomnography or home sleep apnea testing

## Risk Factors

- Obesity (BMI ↑)
- Male sex
- Age >50
- Neck circumference >17" (men), >16" (women)
- Hypertension
- Snoring

## Clinical Features

- Loud snoring
- Witnessed apneas
- Choking/gasping during sleep
- Morning headaches
- Excessive daytime sleepiness
- Nocturia

## Diagnosis

- **Home Sleep Apnea Testing (HSAT)**: In uncomplicated cases
- **Polysomnography (PSG)**: Gold standard; indicated when HSAT inconclusive or suspected complex sleep disorders

## Treatment

- **CPAP** (Continuous Positive Airway Pressure): First-line for moderate-severe OSA
- **Mandibular advancement devices**: Mild-moderate OSA, CPAP-intolerant
- **Weight loss**: Adjunct in all cases
- **Positional therapy**: For positional OSA
