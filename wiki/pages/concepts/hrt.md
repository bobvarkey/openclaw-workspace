# HRT (Hormone Replacement Therapy)

## Definition

Treatment for menopausal symptoms using exogenous hormones — typically estrogen alone (in women without a uterus) or estrogen + progestogen (in women with an intact uterus).

## Indications

- Vasomotor symptoms (hot flashes, night sweats)
- Genitourinary syndrome of menopause (GSM / vaginal atrophy)
- Mood changes related to menopause
- Osteoporosis prevention (when other treatments not suitable)

## Types of Estrogen

| Type | Route | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| **Transdermal estradiol** | Patch, gel, spray | Preferred — bypasses first-pass liver, no increased VTE |
| **Oral estradiol** | Pill | Undergoes hepatic first-pass; higher VTE risk |
| **Conjugated equine estrogen (CEE)** | Oral | Used in WHI; higher VTE risk |

## Types of Progestogen

| Type | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Micronized progesterone** | Better safety profile; natural; Prometrium |
| **Synthetic progestins** | MPA (medroxyprogesterone acetate) — used in WHI; higher risks |

## VTE Risk Summary

| Preparation | VTE Risk per 10,000 women-years |
|-------------|:-------------------------------:|
| Transdermal E2 + micronized P4 | **No increase** (baseline: 2–10) |
| Oral E2 + micronized P4 | 37 |
| Oral CEE + MPA (Prempro) | 53 |

## Key Principle

**Transdermal ≠ Oral.** Modern HRT (transdermal E2 + micronized P4) does NOT increase VTE risk. WHI study used oral Prempro — not applicable to modern regimens.
