# CJD Mimics: Autoimmune Encephalitis and Toxic Encephalopathy

Rapidly progressive dementia with myoclonus and typical MRI/spinal fluid findings can mimic Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease (CJD). Several conditions produce an identical clinical picture.

---

## Anti-AMPAR Encephalitis (Durbano et al., Ann Neurol)

**Key reference:** Durbano et al. — *Creutzfeldt-Jakob-Like Presentation in Anti-AMPAR Encephalitis* — Annals of Neurology, Wiley Online Library

### Clinical Features That Mimic CJD
- Rapidly progressive dementia (weeks to months)
- Myoclonus
- Visual symptoms / cortical blindness
- Cerebellar signs
- Epileptic seizures

### Distinguishing Features
- **CSF anti-GluA1 AMPAR antibodies** (neuronal surface antibody)
- Often associated with **thymoma** or other neoplasms
- May have preceding fever or infection prodrome
- MRI: T2/FLAIR hyperintensities in medial temporal lobes and basal ganglia (similar to CJD)
- EEG: Periodic sharp waves can resemble CJD

### Key Distinction from CJD
| Feature | Anti-AMPAR Encephalitis | CJD |
|---------|------------------------|-----|
| Progression | Subacute (weeks-months) | Subacute (weeks-months) |
| CSF 14-3-3 | May be positive | Usually positive |
| EEG | May have periodic sharp waves | Periodic sharp waves common |
|MRI | Hippocampal + basal ganglia T2/FLAIR | DWI cortical ribboning, basal ganglia |
| Antibody testing | Positive | Negative |
| Treatment response | Immunotherapy responsive | Not responsive |

### Treatment
- First-line: **Corticosteroids**, IVIG, plasma exchange (PLEX)
- Second-line: Rituximab, cyclophosphamide
- Tumor resection if paraneoplastic

## Bismuth Toxicity

Bismuth compounds (bismuth subsalicylate, bismuth subcitrate) in high doses or chronic use can produce a **CJD-like picture**.

### Clinical Features
- Rapidly progressive dementia
- Myoclonus (characteristic)
- Ataxia / gait disturbance
- Tremor (titubation)
- Dysarthria / mutism
- Visual disturbances / cortical blindness
- Confusion, personality change
- Seizures

### Mechanism
- Neurotoxicity from bismuth crossing blood-brain barrier
- Mitochondrial dysfunction, oxidative stress
- Typically with prolonged high-dose use or renal impairment

### MRI Findings
- T2/FLAIR hyperintensities in basal ganglia (putamen > caudate)
- Cerebral cortex involvement
- May show diffusion restriction

### EEG
- Diffuse slowing
- May show periodic complexes (mimicking CJD)

### Key Distinction from CJD
| Feature | Bismuth Toxicity | CJD |
|---------|-------------------|-----|
| History | Chronic bismuth use, renal impairment | No exposure history |
| Progression | May stabilize with bismuth withdrawal | Progressive |
| Myoclonus | Very characteristic | Present |
| MRI basal ganglia | Hyperintensities, may be symmetric | Often asymmetric |
| Bismuth levels | Elevated in blood/urine | Normal |
| Treatment | Chelation, bismuth cessation | No disease-modifying treatment |

### Reversibility
Unlike CJD, bismuth toxicity is **partially or fully reversible** with:
- Immediate discontinuation of bismuth
- Chelation therapy (dimercaprol, DMPS)
- Supportive care
- Recovery may take weeks to months

## Other CJD Mimics to Consider

| Condition | Key distinguishing feature |
|-----------|---------------------------|
| **电压门控钾通道抗体 encephalitis (VGKC)** | LGI1, Caspr2 antibodies |
| **GABA-BR encephalitis** | Small cell lung cancer association |
| **Paraneoplastic cerebellar degeneration** | Cancer association (breast, ovarian, SCLC) |
| **Hashimoto encephalopathy** | Elevated thyroid antibodies, response to steroids |
| **Voltage-gated calcium channel antibodies** | Lambert-Eaton overlap |
| **Meningoencephalitis (infectious)** | HSV, VZV, enterovirus |
| **NMDAR encephalitis** | Young women, ovarian teratoma, distinctive EEG |
| **Metabolic encephalopathies** | Uremia, hepatic failure, CO2 retention |

## Clinical Approach When CJD is Suspected

1. **MRI brain** with DWI — CJD shows cortical ribboning and basal ganglia hyperintensities
2. **CSF 14-3-3 protein** — positive in CJD but also in other encephalitides
3. **CSF Neuronal Surface Antibodies** — anti-AMPAR, anti-NMDAR, anti-VGKC, anti-GABA-BR
4. **Autoimmune encephalitis panel** (serum + CSF)
5. **Review medication history** — bismuth, heavy metals, chemotherapy
6. **Consider trial of immunotherapy** if any red flags for autoimmune etiology
