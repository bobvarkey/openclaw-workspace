# Hypoglossal Nerve (CN XII)

## Anatomy

The hypoglossal nerve provides motor innervation to all intrinsic and most extrinsic muscles of the tongue (except palatoglossus). It is purely motor.

## Hypoglossal Nerve Conduction Study

### Traditional Method
Tongue electrodes were used but suffered from:
- Saliva interference causing signal artifact
- Difficulty maintaining stable contact
- Patient discomfort

### Novel Submental Method (Oliveira et al. 2026)
- Active electrode: submental region (under the chin)
- Reference electrode: contralateral side
- Eliminates tongue electrode entirely

### Normative Data (34 healthy adults)
| Parameter | Mean | Upper Limit |
|-----------|------|-------------|
| CMAP onset latency | 1.27 ms | 1.58 ms |
| Amplitude | — | >0.88 mV |
| Decremental response | -0.53% | — |

## Clinical Applications

- Motor neuron disease (ALS) — tongue weakness is an early sign
- Bulbar palsy assessment
- Post-surgical hypoglossal nerve monitoring
- Cranial nerve neuropathy workup
