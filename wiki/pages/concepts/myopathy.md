# Myopathy

## Definition

A disease of skeletal muscle characterized by muscle fiber dysfunction. Myopathies cause weakness, typically proximal, with characteristic EMG and muscle biopsy findings.

## Classification

| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| **Inflammatory** | Dermatomyositis, polymyositis, inclusion body myositis |
| **Metabolic** | McArdle disease, carnitine deficiency |
| **Muscular Dystrophies** | Duchenne, Becker, limb-girdle |
| **Congenital** | Central core disease, nemaline myopathy |
| **Endocrine** | Thyroid myopathy, Cushing myopathy |
| **Drug-induced** | Statin myopathy, corticosteroid myopathy |

## Clinical Features

- **Proximal weakness** — difficulty climbing stairs, rising from chair, raising arms
- **Symmetric** presentation (typically)
- **No sensory loss**
- **Normal reflexes** (early); may be reduced late
- **Myalgia** (in inflammatory myopathies)
- **CK elevation** (variable)

## Diagnostic Workup

| Test | Finding |
|------|---------|
| **Muscle ultrasound** | Increased echogenicity (Heckmatt grade) |
| **EMG** | Short duration, low amplitude MUAPs; early recruitment |
| **CK** | Often elevated |
| **MRI** | Muscle edema, fatty replacement |
| **Biopsy** | Fiber necrosis, regeneration, specific pathological features |

## Myopathy vs Neuropathy

| Feature | Myopathy | Neuropathy |
|---------|----------|------------|
| Pattern | Proximal, symmetric | Distal, often asymmetric |
| Sensory | Normal | Often abnormal |
| Reflexes | Normal/reduced | Reduced/absent |
| EMG | Myopathic MUAPs | Neurogenic MUAPs |
| CK | Often elevated | Usually normal |
