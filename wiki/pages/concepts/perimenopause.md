# Perimenopause

## Definition

The menopause transition — the period from onset of menstrual irregularity through the first year after the final menstrual period (FMP).

## Timeline

| Stage | Timing |
|-------|--------|
| **Early perimenopause** | Menstrual cycles may be regular or slightly irregular |
| **Late perimenopause** | 1–2 years **before** FMP to 2 years **after** FMP |
| **Menopause** | 12 months amenorrhea |
| **Postmenopause** | After 12 months amenorrhea |

## Key Clinical Features

- **Menstrual irregularity** — variable cycle length (>7 days different from normal)
- **Vasomotor symptoms** — hot flashes, night sweats
- **Sleep disturbances**
- **Mood changes**

## Critical Window: Late Perimenopause

**Peak bone loss occurs during late perimenopause** — specifically 1–2 years before FMP to 2 years after FMP.

### Why This Matters

- Bone loss **accelerates** during late perimenopause due to estrogen decline
- This is the **optimal window for intervention** with HRT
- Missing this window may result in irreversible bone density loss

## Bone Health Implications

- **Estrogen prevents bone loss**
- HRT initiated during late perimenopause preserves bone density
- [[Osteoporosis]] prevention is most effective when started during this window
