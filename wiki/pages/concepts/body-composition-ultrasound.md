# Body Composition Ultrasound

## Definition

Using ultrasound to assess body composition — specifically muscle mass, subcutaneous fat, and organ dimensions as indicators of nutritional status and metabolic health.

## Key Assessments

### Quadriceps Assessment
| Parameter | Clinical Use |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Subcutaneous fat thickness** | Adiposity assessment |
| **Muscle thickness** | Muscle mass indicator |
| **Muscle-to-fat ratio** | Muscle quality |

### Spleen Ultrasound
**Splenomegaly indicators:**

| Finding | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Normal spleen size | ≤12 cm |
| Splenomegaly ratio | Spleen:kidney length >1.25 |
| Shape | Rounder than oval |

**Causes of Organomegaly (large kidney on US):**
- Diabetes mellitus
- Amyloidosis
- Lymphoma
- HIV

## Clinical Applications

### Nutritional Assessment
- Muscle mass depletion (sarcopenia)
- Adipose tissue distribution
- Organomegaly

### Systemic Disease Indicators
- HIV → lymphadenopathy, splenomegaly
- Lymphoma → organomegaly
- DM → kidney size changes
- Amyloidosis → organ infiltration

## Advantages of Ultrasound

- No radiation
- Bedside/portable
- Real-time
- No BMI limitations (unlike BIA)
- Longitudinal monitoring possible
