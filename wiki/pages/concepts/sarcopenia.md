# Sarcopenia

## Definition

Age-related loss of skeletal muscle mass, strength, and function. A major cause of functional decline, frailty, and loss of independence in older adults.

## Assessment with Muscle Ultrasound

### Quadriceps Muscle Ultrasound
Primary muscle assessed for sarcopenia:

| Parameter | What It Indicates |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Quadriceps muscle thickness** | Muscle mass |
| **Subcutaneous fat** | Adipose deposition |
| Thickness-to-fat ratio | Muscle quality |

### Muscle Thickness Measurement
- Quadriceps (typically rectus femoris) thickness measured at standardized landmark
- Compared to age-adjusted reference values
- Longitudinal monitoring for decline

## Clinical Significance

- Predictor of:
  - Falls
  - Hospitalization
  - Post-operative complications
  - Mortality
  - Loss of independence

## Diagnostic Criteria (EWGSOP)

| Criterion | Measurement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Muscle mass | Appendicular skeletal muscle mass / height² |
| Muscle strength | Grip strength or chair stand test |
| Physical performance | Gait speed, SPPB, TUG |

## Prevention & Treatment

- **Resistance exercise** — most effective intervention
- **Protein supplementation** — 1.0–1.2 g/kg/day
- **Vitamin D** supplementation if deficient
- **Anabolic agents** (in selected cases)
