# Phrenic Nerve

## Anatomy

The phrenic nerve (C3, C4, C5) provides the sole motor innervation to the diaphragm — the primary muscle of respiration.

## Phrenic Nerve Conduction Study (NCS)

### Purpose
Assess phrenic nerve function to evaluate diaphragm compromise in neuromuscular diseases.

### Technique
- Stimulation: posterior border of sternocleidomastoid at the level of the cricoid cartilage
- Recording: CMAP over the diaphragm (typically at the nipple line or 7th intercostal space)

### Recent Normative Data (Castro & de Carvalho 2026)
Largest dataset: 132 healthy subjects aged 23–90 years

**Key findings:**
- Latency increases with age: ~+0.25 ms/decade
- Men have higher amplitudes than women
- Left-sided CMAP amplitudes are significantly larger

**Abnormal thresholds:**
| | Men | Women |
|---|---|---|
| Latency | >9.5 ms | >8.5 ms |
| Amplitude | <0.62 mV | <0.48 mV |

## Clinical Applications

- ALS assessment (diaphragm weakness is common)
- Guillain-Barré syndrome
- Neuromuscular junction disorders
- Post-surgical diaphragm dysfunction
- Respiratory failure workup
