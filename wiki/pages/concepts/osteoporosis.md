# Osteoporosis

## Definition

A skeletal disorder characterized by compromised bone strength predisposing to increased fracture risk. Defined by WHO as:
- **T-score ≤ −2.5** at lumbar spine, total hip, or femoral neck on DXA scan

## Bone Mineral Density (BMD)

| Category | T-score |
|----------|---------|
| Normal | ≥ −1.0 |
| Osteopenia | −1.0 to −2.5 |
| Osteoporosis | ≤ −2.5 |

## Osteoporosis in Women

### Key Risk Period: Perimenopause
- **Peak bone loss occurs during perimenopause**
- Specifically: **1–2 years before final menstrual period (FMP)** to **2 years after FMP**
- This represents a **critical window** for intervention

### Estrogen and Bone
- Estrogen deficiency → increased osteoclast activity → accelerated bone resorption
- Replacing estrogen during perimenopause/menopause **prevents bone loss**

## Estradiol Doses for Bone Protection

### Oral Estradiol

| Dose | BMD Effect (2 years) |
|------|---------------------|
| 0.5 mg | Femoral neck BMD ↑3.8%; prevents spine bone loss |
| 1 mg | Lumbar spine BMD ↑5.2% |
| 2 mg | Lumbar spine BMD ↑6.7% |

### Transdermal Estradiol

| Dose | BMD Effect (2 years) |
|------|---------------------|
| 0.025 mg | Lumbar spine BMD ↑2.37% |
| 0.05 mg | Lumbar spine BMD ↑4.09% |
| 0.1 mg | Lumbar spine BMD ↑4.70% |

## Prevention Strategies

- **HRT during perimenopause/menopause** — most effective
- Calcium and vitamin D
- Weight-bearing exercise
- Fall prevention
- Bisphosphonates (if HRT contraindicated or insufficient)
