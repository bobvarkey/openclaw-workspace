# EMG Interpretation

## Definition

EMG (Electromyography) interpretation refers to the analysis of needle electrode examination (NEE) findings — assessing electrical activity in muscles to diagnose neuromuscular disorders.

## Components of EMG/NCS Examination

| Component | What It Evaluates |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Nerve conduction studies (NCS) | Peripheral nerve function (speed, amplitude) |
| Needle EMG | Muscle electrical activity at rest and during contraction |
| F-waves | Proximal nerve segments |
| H-reflexes | Spinal reflex arcs |

## Key EMG Patterns

### Normal
- Silence at rest (except endplate noise)
- Full interference pattern on volition

### Neurogenic
- Fibrillation potentials, positive sharp waves (denervation)
- Fasciculation potentials
- Reduced interference pattern
- High amplitude, long duration motor unit potentials

### Myopathic
- Short duration, low amplitude motor units
- Early recruitment, full interference pattern
- No fibrillations (usually)
