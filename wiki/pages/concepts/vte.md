# VTE (Venous Thromboembolism)

## Definition

Blood clot forming in a vein — includes deep vein thrombosis (DVT) and pulmonary embolism (PE). A major safety consideration for hormonal therapies.

## VTE Risk Context

| Scenario | VTE Risk per 10,000 Women-Years |
|----------|:-------------------------------:|
| Baseline (reproductive-aged women) | 2–10 |
| Transdermal estradiol + micronized P4 | **No increase** |
| Oral estradiol + micronized P4 | 37 |
| Oral CEE + MPA (Prempro) | 53 |
| Pregnancy (third trimester) | 114 |
| Postpartum (first 3 weeks) | 421 |

## Why Oral Estrogen Increases VTE Risk

Oral estrogen undergoes **first-pass hepatic metabolism**:
- Increases hepatic synthesis of clotting factors (I, VII, IX, X)
- Increases fibrinogen levels
- Decreases antithrombin III
- Effect is dose and duration dependent

**Transdermal estrogen bypasses the liver** → no increased clotting risk

## Risk-Benefit Considerations

- VTE risk from oral HRT is still lower than pregnancy risk
- For women with history of VTE or thrombophilia: transdermal is generally preferred
- Individual risk assessment (BMI, smoking, family history) is essential
