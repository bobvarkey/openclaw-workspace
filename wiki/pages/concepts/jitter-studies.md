# Jitter Studies

## Definition

Jitter studies measure the variability in neuromuscular transmission at individual motor end-plates. They are the most sensitive test for **neuromuscular junction (NMJ) disorders**.

## What Is Jitter?

Jitter = the variation in time between two consecutive muscle fiber depolarizations (expressed in microseconds, µs).

- **Low jitter** = stable NMJ transmission
- **High jitter** = impaired NMJ transmission

## Measurement Methods

| Method | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **SFEMG** (Single Fiber EMG) | Gold standard; records from single muscle fibers |
| **CNE** (Concentric Needle Electrode) | Records from a small group of fibers; more clinically practical |
| **Stimulated** | Nerve is electrically stimulated; more controlled |
| **Volitional** | Patient activates muscle voluntarily |

## Clinical Applications

- **Myasthenia Gravis** — most common indication
- **Lambert-Eaton Myasthenic Syndrome (LEMS)**
- **Botulism**
- **Ocular MG** (early/negative antibody cases)

## Key Reference Values (CNE, µs)

| Muscle | Mean UL | Individual UL |
|--------|:-------:|:------------:|
| Orbicularis oculi (vol) | 31 | 45 |
| Orbicularis oculi (stim) | 27 | 36 |
| Frontalis (vol) | 28 | 38 |
| Frontalis (stim) | 21 | 28 |
| Extensor digitorum (vol) | 30 | 43 |
| Extensor digitorum (stim) | 24 | 35 |

## Interpretation

- **Abnormal**: Mean/median exceeds upper limit OR >10% of individual pairs abnormal
- **Exclude**: Jitter <5 µs (artifactual)
- **For jitter >150 µs**: Use median (more robust than mean)
