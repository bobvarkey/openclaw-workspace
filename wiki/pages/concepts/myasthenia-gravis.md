# Myasthenia Gravis (MG)

## Definition

An autoimmune disorder causing antibodies against acetylcholine receptors (AChR) or MuSK proteins at the neuromuscular junction, leading to fatigable muscle weakness.

## Clinical Features

- **Fatigable weakness** — worsens with activity, improves with rest
- **Ocular**: ptosis, diplopia (most common initial presentation)
- **Bulbar**: dysphagia, dysarthria, chewing fatigue
- **Limb weakness**: proximal > distal
- **Respiratory**: diaphragm weakness (can be life-threatening)

## Diagnosis

| Test | What It Shows |
|------|---------------|
| **Anti-AChR antibodies** | Positive in ~85% of generalized MG |
| **Anti-MuSK antibodies** | Positive in ~40% of AChR-negative MG |
| **Edrophonium (Tensilon) test** | Rapid but brief improvement with IV edrophonium |
| **Ice pack test** | Improves ptosis (for ocular MG) |
| **Jitter studies (SFEMG/CNE)** | Abnormal jitter even when EMG/NCS normal |

## Jitter Study Findings in MG

- **Most sensitive test** for MG — abnormal even when routine EMG is normal
- Elevated jitter with blocking
- Particularly useful in seronegative MG (antibody-negative but clinically suspected)

## Reference Values (CNE Jitter)

See [[Jitter Studies]] for muscle-specific upper limit values.

## Treatment

- **Acetylcholinesterase inhibitors**: Pyridostigmine (symptomatic)
- **Immunosuppression**: Prednisone, azathioprine, rituximab, eculizumab
- **Crisis**: IVIG, plasmapheresis
