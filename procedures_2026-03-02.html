RMA Blood-Based Brain Activity Monitoring Source: Neuron (Rice University / Emory) Novel Technique: Released Markers of Activity (RMAs) - engineered proteins crossing blood-brain barrier Clinical Outcomes: • Detects activity of 10-100 neurons (vs 100,000+ for MRI) • Successful in rhesus macaques - primate translation complete • Longitudinal monitoring possible via simple blood draws Implementation: • Requires gene therapy delivery of RMA construct • Blood sample analyzed via mass spectrometry or single-molecule protein sequencing • Can multiplex - track multiple genes/brain regions simultaneously Patient Selection: Ideal for research in addiction, Huntington's, Alzheimer's - diseases requiring longitudinal monitoring India Relevance: Partnership opportunities with Indian neuroscience institutes; could reduce need for repeated PET scans --- 2. Tau PET Scan Interpretation Refinement Source: Acta Neuropathologica (UCSF) Novel Finding: Flortaucipir tracer binds to iron deposits and MAO-B (neuroinflammation), not just tau Clinical Outcomes: • AI voxel-by-voxel matching reveals "off-target" binding in non-Alzheimer's tauopathies • Helps avoid over-interpretation of borderline tau PET signals Implementation: • Clinicians should consider iron/inflammation in differential when tau PET is positive but clinical picture unclear • Critical for patient selection in anti-tau drug trials India Relevance: Improving diagnostic accuracy for Indian memory clinics using tau PET
