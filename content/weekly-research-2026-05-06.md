## 1. BCI Stroke Rehabilitation — Major RCT Data

### CorTec Brain Interchange™ — FDA Breakthrough Designation
**Source:** CorTec GmbH (April 8, 2026)

CorTec received FDA Breakthrough Device Designation for its Brain Interchange™ system — the **first BCI worldwide designated specifically for stroke motor rehabilitation**.

- Uses direct cortical electrical stimulation to support motor recovery
- Fully implantable BCI
- Targets chronic stroke patients with upper extremity motor deficit

**Clinical implication:** This is a regulatory milestone. FDA's Breakthrough designation means priority review and a clearer path to market. Stroke rehabilitation BCI just moved from "promising research" to "imminent product."

---

### IpsiHand BCI-Enabled Therapy — RCT at ISC 2026
**Source:** Eric Leuthardt, ISC 2026 (late-breaking oral abstract)

Post-market RCT of at-home BCI-enabled therapy with IpsiHand (device detects motor intent from contralateral hemisphere via EEG, triggers functional electrical stimulation on affected limb):

- Significant functional outcome improvement vs standard exercise therapy
- Chronic upper-extremity deficit post-stroke
- At-home, not clinic-based
- First RCT-level evidence for BCI stroke rehab at scale

**Clinical implication:** Motor recovery after stroke may no longer be limited to the acute/subacute window. If at-home BCI therapy works at scale, this changes rehabilitation economics entirely — no clinic visits, no therapist present during sessions.

## 2. AI in Stroke Diagnosis — Real-World Deployment Data

### AI-Enabled Acute Stroke Triage — JNNP 2026
**Source:** Journal of Neurology, Neurosurgery & Psychiatry (March 27, 2026)

Prospective database review in a large academic network — before/after AI-enabled triage platform implementation:

- Evaluated workflow efficiency and transfer optimization
- AI triage system for acute ischemic stroke
- Compared equivalent time periods pre/post implementation

**Clinical implication:** If AI triage meaningfully reduces door-to-groin or door-to-needle times, this is the strongest argument yet for integrated AI stroke protocols. Every 10-minute improvement in thrombectomy door-to-reperfusion = ~10% reduction in disability-adjusted life years.

---

### ML for Thrombectomy Outcome Prediction — PCAO
**Source:** The Neuroradiology Journal / Springer (2026)

Machine learning models predicting functional outcomes after thrombectomy for **posterior circulation acute occlusion (PCAO)**:

- Good diagnostic accuracy for predicting post-thrombectomy outcomes
- Could support individualized decision-making and resource allocation
- Prospective validation still needed before routine clinical use

**Clinical implication:** Not all large vessel occlusion thrombectomy patients benefit equally. ML models that predict who will regain independence vs remain dependent could prevent futile procedures and reduce healthcare costs — while also giving patients honest prognoses.

---

### AI Diagnostic Accuracy — Ischemic vs Hemorrhagic Stroke
**Source:** PMC (2026)

AI-based systems showing high diagnostic accuracy for both ischemic and hemorrhagic stroke — closely approaching human radiologist performance:

- Both stroke types: AI systems match radiologist sensitivity/specificity
- Potential to expedite clinical decision-making in acute settings
- System-level deployment across multiple hospital networks

**Clinical implication:** AI in stroke diagnosis is past the "will it work?" phase. The question is now "where do you deploy it?" ER triage? Concurrent read? Night coverage? The answer is all three, and the data supports it.
