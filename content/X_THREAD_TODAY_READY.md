**Tweet 2**
> For decades, neuroscientists struggled with a puzzle:
> 
> Deep learning trains via backprop — the network computes an error signal and sends it backward through layers to adjust each weight.
> 
> But the brain? No global error signal they could find.
> 
> Until now.

---

**Tweet 3**
> MIT researchers found that during learning, specific neurons receive *neuron-specific feedback signals* — not a one-size-fits-all broadcast.
> 
> Each cell gets told: "You were off by X. Adjust accordingly."
> 
> Sound familiar? That's EXACTLY what backprop does.
> 
> #DeepLearning

---

**Tweet 4**
> Here's the wild part:
> 
> This contradicts the long-held view that brains only use local learning rules (Hebbian plasticity, STDP).
> 
> The evidence suggests the cortex implements *credit assignment* — finding which neurons caused errors and fixing them.
