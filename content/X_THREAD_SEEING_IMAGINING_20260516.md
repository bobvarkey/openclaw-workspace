# X Thread Seeing Imagining 20260516

1/8

Your brain doesn't create mental images.

It LITERALLY sees them. 🧵

A landmark study just dropped in Science, and it changes everything we thought we knew about imagination.

Let me explain 👇

2/8

For the first time ever, scientists recorded from SINGLE neurons in the human brain while people imagined things.

The result?

The exact same neurons in the ventral temporal cortex fire — identically — whether you SEE an apple or simply IMAGINE one.

Same cells. Same patterns. 🧠

3/8

This was led by Ueli Rutishauser at Cedars-Sinai, as part of the Simons Collaboration on the Global Brain.

Published in Science (doi: 10.1126/science.adt8343)

They weren't just looking at brain regions lighting up on an fMRI. They recorded individual neurons firing in real time.

4/8

Think about what this means.

When you close your eyes and picture your childhood home, your brain isn't pulling up a rough sketch or a symbolic placeholder.

It's reconstructing a full sensory experience — using the same neural machinery as actual vision.

5/8

This is what neuroscientists call a "generative model."

Your brain takes a tiny conceptual seed — "apple" — and runs a full synthesis routine, generating the color, shape, texture, everything — from scratch.

It doesn't retrieve an image. It BUILDS one.

6/8

Here's where it gets wild 🚀

This is basically what diffusion models (Midjourney, Sora, Stable Diffusion) do.

They take a prompt — a conceptual seed — and denoise random pixels into a coherent image.

The brain and AI are converging on the same computational principle.
