**Tweet 3/7**

2/ Allen Institute — Denis Turcu is using MICrONS, the largest synaptic map of a mammalian brain ever built, to teach AI to learn like a real brain.

His insight? Different synapses use DIFFERENT learning rules — local vs global — not one-size-fits-all.

Biology has been doing MoE before it was cool. 🧬🤖

**Tweet 4/7**

3/ Google Research — MoGen generates synthetic neuron geometries using AI, then uses them to train better brain-mapping models.

Result: 4.4% fewer reconstruction errors.

At the scale of a full mouse brain? That's 157 PERSON-YEARS of manual proofreading saved. 🔬💥

research.google/blog/ai-generated-synthetic-neurons-speed-up-brain-mapping/

**Tweet 5/7**

4/ Princeton — They built a 3D device that combines LIVING brain cells with flexible electronics into a working biological neural network.

It's not a simulation. These are real neurons growing through an electronic mesh, trained with electrical pulses to recognize patterns.

Wetware computing just went 3D. 🧫🔌

**Tweet 6/7**

5/ Cleveland Clinic — GenT is an AI framework that analyzes entire genes (not just individual mutations) to find drug targets for brain disorders.

It identified 16 genes for Alzheimer's, 15 for ALS, 35 for major depression, and 83 for schizophrenia.

GenT validated NTRK1 as a druggable target for Alzheimer's — confirmed in patient neurons. 💊🧪
