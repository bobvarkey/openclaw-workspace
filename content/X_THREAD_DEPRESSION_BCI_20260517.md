# X Thread: Motif Neurotech — First Therapeutic BCI for Depression

**Tweet 1/8**

The first BCI for depression just got FDA approval for human trials.

Not for moving cursors. Not for typing with thought.

For changing your mood.

Motif Neurotech's DOT implant is blueberry-sized, sits in your skull, and targets the brain circuit that goes dormant in depression. 🧠⚡

---

**Tweet 2/8**

Meet the DOT — Digitally programmable Over-brain Therapeutic.

It's a wirelessly powered implant the size of a blueberry, placed in the skull just above the dura (the brain's protective membrane).

No brain surgery. No wires through the skull. A 20-minute outpatient procedure.

It targets the Central Executive Network — the part of your brain responsible for planning, focus, and motivation. In major depression, this network is chronically underactive.

---

**Tweet 3/8**

Here's how it works:

The implant delivers specific patterns of electrical stimulation — called "motifs" — to turn ON the central executive network.

Patients wear a baseball cap that wirelessly powers and controls the device. They get 10-20 minute sessions, multiple times a day.

The goal? Drive neuroplasticity. Strengthen the connections that depression has weakened.

Think of it as a pacemaker — for your brain's motivation circuit.

---

**Tweet 4/8**

The expectation from the company:

Patients could show meaningful response — even remission — within the first 10 days of therapy.

After that, maintenance sessions become less frequent.

Compare this to TMS (transcranial magnetic stimulation), the current gold standard: 5 sessions per week for 6 weeks.

DOT is implanted once, and treatment happens at home.

---

**Tweet 5/8**

Why this matters:

Nearly 3 million Americans have treatment-resistant depression — meaning antidepressants haven't worked after trying multiple medications.

Current options are limited: ECT (shock therapy), TMS (requires daily clinic visits for 6 weeks), or experimental deep brain stimulation.

None of these involve a BCI. None of them let patients self-administer at home.

DOT is the first brain-computer interface designed specifically for mental health.

---

**Tweet 6/8**

The company behind it: Motif Neurotech, founded in 2022 out of Rice University.

CEO Jacob Robinson spent over a decade developing the magnetoelectric wireless power technology that makes the implant work.

The FDA approved their investigational device exemption in just 4 years — a record timeline for a BCI company.

They've also been selected for ARPA-H's EVIDENT program to study precision neuromodulation.
