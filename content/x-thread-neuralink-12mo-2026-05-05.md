# X Thread: Neuralink's 12-Month Human Trial Results

**Angle:** "Neuralink just dropped 12-month human trial data — here's what happened to 5 paralyzed patients"

**Date:** May 5, 2026

---

**Tweet 1/8 🧵**

Neuralink just published one-year outcomes from its PRIME study — five people with tetraplegia living with the N1 implant.

Here's what the data actually shows, what went wrong, and what it means for the timeline of brain-computer interfaces.

Let's dive in.

---

**Tweet 2/8**

The trial started in January 2024 with Noland Arbaugh, a 29-year-old quadriplegic since a 2016 diving accident.

Within days, he was controlling a computer cursor with his thoughts — playing chess, browsing the web, making video calls.

But then the hardware hit a wall.

---

**Tweet 3/8**

Within a month of implant, ~85% of Arbaugh's electrode threads retracted from brain tissue.

Tissue shifts and healing caused the polymer threads — thinner than a human hair — to pull away.

Neuralink's fix: a software patch that redistributed signal processing across remaining electrodes.

It worked. But it was a workaround, not a fix.

---

**Tweet 4/8**

Fast forward to today: 5 patients, 670+ cumulative days of implant time, 4,900+ hours of active use.

The results:

→ All 5 can control cursors by thought
→ Multiple patients type independently
→ One patient (Brad Smith, ALS) produced and edited a YouTube video using thought + eye tracking, narrated by an AI reconstruction of his own voice
→ 3D CAD design demonstrated

These aren't lab demos — this is daily life.

---

**Tweet 5/8**

The surgical advancement that matters most:

Neuralink's R1 robot can now insert electrode threads through the dura mater without removing it.

This is a genuine breakthrough. The dura is the brain's tough outer membrane — traditional approaches open it up, adding infection risk and recovery time.

The new method: punch through. Shorter surgery, lower risk, faster recovery.

---

**Tweet 6/8**

Scaling is real.

→ ~20 patients implanted globally by late 2025
→ UK's GB-PRIME enrolled 7 patients in ~10 weeks
→ UAE-PRIME now recruiting at Cleveland Clinic Abu Dhabi
→ 10,000+ on the US waiting list
→ $650M raised in Series E (June 2025)
→ High-volume N1 production + automated surgery planned for 2026

The bottleneck is no longer the tech — it's manufacturing and regulatory.

---

**Tweet 7/8**

Let's be honest about the limitations:

→ Only 5 patients is a case series, not a product launch
→ Electrode thread retraction was a real setback — future patients need lasting signal stability
→ Neuralink has not yet published peer-reviewed clinical data (a GB-PRIME paper is expected in the next 12 months)
→ Animal welfare concerns from earlier primate testing remain unresolved

BCIs work. But the evidence needs to catch up to the hype.

---

**Tweet 8/8**

The bottom line:

A quadriplegic playing chess with his mind and an ALS patient editing video with thought alone were science fiction 5 years ago.

Today they're patients in a clinical trial.

The path from 20 implants to 20,000 is long. But for the first time, it looks real.
