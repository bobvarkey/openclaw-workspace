# YouTube Short: "3 Signs a Stroke Is Coming"

**Target:** 60 seconds, ~170 words
**Audience:** AI + Neurology enthusiasts, medical professionals, health-conscious general audience
**Tone:** Urgent, practical, shareable

---

## 0–5s | Hook

A stroke kills 2 million brain cells *every minute*. Most people miss the warning signs until it's too late. Here are 3 signs your brain is in danger — right now.

## 5–20s | Sign 1 — Balance & Vision (B.E.)

**Balance loss:** Suddenly can't stand straight? Feel like the room is spinning? That's not vertigo — it could be a posterior circulation stroke.

**Eye changes:** Blurred vision, double vision, or sudden vision loss in one eye. When both hit together, the clot may already be in your brainstem.

## 20–38s | Sign 2 — Face & Arm (F.A.)

**Face drooping:** Ask them to smile. One side doesn't move? That's facial nerve damage caused by interrupted blood flow.

**Arm weakness:** Raise both arms. One drifts down? That's motor cortex ischemia — and you're on the clock.

## 38–50s | Sign 3 — Speech & Time (S.T.)

**Speech difficulty:** Slurred words, can't repeat a simple sentence, or suddenly can't understand what you're saying. That's the language centers starving for oxygen.

**Time:** Symptoms came and went? That's a transient ischemic attack — a "mini-stroke." 1 in 3 who have one will have a major stroke within a year.

## 50–60s | CTA

Know the checklist: **B.E. F.A.S.T.** — Balance, Eyes, Face, Arm, Speech, Time. Memorize it. Share it. It could save someone you love.

Drop a 🧠 if you learned something — and tag someone who needs to see this.
