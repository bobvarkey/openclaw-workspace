**Brain hemorrhage on CT:**
A 2024 JAMA Neurology paper: AI triage flagged intracranial hemorrhage with 97.1% sensitivity, 95.5% specificity.

The model detected bleeds that initial human reads missed — particularly in the posterior fossa and small subdural bleeds.

Multiple US health systems now run AI concurrent read on all head CTs.

**Tweet 4/8:**
**Breast cancer on mammography:**
A 2025 NEJM study (Sweden, 80,000 women): AI-supported screening reduced false positives by 5.7% while maintaining cancer detection rates.

The AI didn't replace the radiologist. It made the radiologist more accurate.

In real-world deployment, that's thousands of women avoiding unnecessary biopsies.

**Tweet 5/8:**
Why does AI win on specific tasks?

→ Consistent attention — doesn't fatigue after 50 reads
→ Pattern recognition across hundreds of thousands of cases
→ No anchoring bias — doesn't anchor on "this patient is young and healthy"
→ Parallel processing — reviews all images simultaneously, not sequentially

Humans have advantages too. But these are real edges.
