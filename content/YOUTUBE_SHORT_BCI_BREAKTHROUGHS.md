## Final Script (130 words / ~50 sec)

"Three BCI breakthroughs this year that should make headlines — and aren't.

One: China approved the Beinao No.1 implantable BCI in April. First-in-human surgery was live-streamed. A paralyzed patient now types with thought alone. This is not a trial — it's approved medical treatment.

Two: Neuralink's VOICE trial gave an ALS patient his voice back. Kenneth Shock, nonverbal for years, now generates audible speech from neural signals. 21 participants enrolled across four countries. Zero serious complications.

Three: AI can now predict which brain injury patients will recover consciousness. Adversarial models decode hidden neural patterns that human eyes can't see — guiding treatment decisions for families and clinicians.

The BCI era is not coming. It's here. And it's moving faster than most people realize."
