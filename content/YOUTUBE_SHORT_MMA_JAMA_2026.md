## Final Script (145 words / ~55 sec)

"JAMA just published a meta-analysis on middle meningeal artery embolization for chronic subdural hematoma. Here is what every neurointerventionist needs to know.

The analysis looked at multiple large randomized controlled trials comparing MMA embolization — either alone or as an adjunct to surgical evacuation — against surgery alone.

The result: MMA embolization significantly reduces recurrence of chronic SDH. The effect is robust across studies. Recurrence rates dropped by more than half in the embolization groups.

Why does this matter? Because chronic SDH is one of the most common neurosurgical conditions. Recurrence after conventional treatment is frustratingly common — especially in elderly patients on antithrombotics.

MMA embolization addresses the pathophysiology — the neovascular membrane — rather than just the mass effect.

The takeaway: adjunctive MMA embolization is moving from 'promising technique' to evidence-based standard. If you manage subdurals, this changes your algorithm."
