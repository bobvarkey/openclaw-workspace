## Hook (0:00–0:30)
"Every time you learn something new, your brain physically changes. Not metaphorically. Literally. Here's the science — and why it matters more than ever in the age of AI."

## Section 1: What Is Neuroplasticity? (0:30–2:00)
- Definition: brain's ability to reorganize, form new neural pathways
- Types: functional plasticity (rerouting tasks) + structural plasticity (physical changes)
- Key stat: London taxi drivers have larger hippocampi — proof of change through use
- Misconception: adult brain NOT fixed; it's constantly adapting

## Section 2: The Mechanism (2:00–4:30)
- Synaptic pruning: "neurons that fire together wire together"
- Long-term potentiation (LTP): how memories form at the synaptic level
- Myelinization: why practice makes you faster (nerve conduction velocity)
- BDNF: brain's fertilizer — exercise boosts it
- Neurogenesis in hippocampus: adult brains still make new neurons

## Section 3: AI Parallel (4:30–7:00)
- Neural networks were inspired by neuroplasticity
- Weight adjustments = synaptic strengthening
- Fine-tuning = targeted plasticity
- Contrast: biological plasticity is slow/sparse; AI is fast/dense
- Key insight: your brain can't unlearn; AI models can. What does that mean for us?

## Section 4: Practical Applications (7:00–9:00)
- Deliberate practice > passive consumption
- Spaced repetition: sleep consolidates; timing matters
- The 4-hour rule: minimum for measurable brain change? (cite studies)
- Why variety in learning = more plastic engagement
- Failure as signal: errors drive more plastic change than successes

## Section 5: Implications for AI+Neuro (9:00–10:30)
- Brain-computer interfaces: neuroplasticity is both tool and obstacle
- AI as cognitive scaffold — does it change how we think?
- Community angle: building in public + learning in public = compound effect
- Call to action: what will YOU learn this week that will physically rewire your brain?
