# CAR-T Cell Treatment for Stiff Person Syndrome

## Overview
Stiff Person Syndrome (SPS) is a rare autoimmune disorder characterized by muscle stiffness, spasms, and autoantibodies against GAD65 (glutamic acid decarboxylase).

## Key Study
**Journal of Neuroinflammation, March 2026**
- PMID: 41821047
- Title: "CAR T cell therapy in autoantibody-mediated neurological disorders: a promising strategy"

## Mechanism
CAR-T therapy targets B cells that produce pathogenic autoantibodies:
- CD19/20-directed CAR T cells target general B cell-mediated pathogenesis
- CAARs (Chimeric Autoantibody Receptors) selectively deplete pathogenic B cells

## Classification of Target Antigens
| Type | Examples | Conditions |
|------|----------|------------|
| Extracellular | AQP4, AChR, NMDAR, MOG, VGCC | NMO, MG, LEMS, MOG-AD |
| Intracellular | GAD65, DAGLA | Stiff Person Syndrome |
| Unknown | - | Multiple Sclerosis |

## Clinical Application
CAR-T offers:
- Highly specific elimination of autoreactive B cells
- Potential for long-term remission
- More targeted than broad immunosuppression

## Challenges
- Safety concerns
- Neurotoxicity risks
- Long-term follow-up data needed

## Future Directions
- Regulatory T cells modified with CARs
- CAARs for selective B cell depletion
- Combined approaches for refractory cases
