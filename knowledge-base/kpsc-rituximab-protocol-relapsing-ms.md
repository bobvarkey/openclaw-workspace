## Overview

A treatment de-escalation flowchart for Rituximab (RTX) in relapsing Multiple Sclerosis, outlining how to step down dosing over time based on clinical stability, MRI activity, and lab markers.

---

## Treatment Flowchart Summary

### Induction Phase
- **1000mg** initial induction dose
- Followed by **500mg at 6 months** and **12 months**

### De-escalation Steps

| Phase | Duration Stable | Dose | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Initial reduction | 5 years total stable | 500mg | Every 12 months (q12mo) × 3 doses |
| Further reduction | Criteria NOT met | 500mg | Every 18 months (q18mo) × 2 doses |
| Further reduction | Still stable | 500mg | Every 24 months (q24mo) |
| **Treatment cessation** | 10 years stable + age >55 | — | Stop RTX |

### Criteria to Consider Continuing 500mg q12mo (don't de-escalate further)
- High IgG levels (>800 mg/dL)
- B cell counts (CD19+) >100/µL
- Age <40 years
- Belongs to **high-risk group**

### High-Risk Group Definition
Patients are considered high-risk if they have:
- Moderate or worse residual disability from prior relapses
- History of myelopathic relapses with sphincter or motor symptoms
- Significant ambulatory or hand function impairment
- Breakthrough disease activity on a prior disease-modifying therapy (DMT)
- High MRI lesion load at treatment start

---

## Protocol Exceptions

### When to EXTEND/DELAY a Dose
Do not infuse if:
- CD19+ B cell counts <100 cells/µL **AND**
- Patient has low/declining IgG **OR**
- History of serious infection

### When to HOLD/PAUSE Treatment
Stop treatment if:
- Pregnancy (with inactive MS)
- Active infection
- Very low IgG (<500 mg/dL)
- Neutropenia
- Progressive/inactive MS with history of serious infection
- Undergoing cancer treatment

### When to Continue 500mg q6mo (Maintain Higher Frequency)
- Disease activity (relapses or new MRI lesions) despite treatment
- B cells return quickly
- Requires ≥2 years of stability before any dose interval reduction attempt

---

## Key Notes
- De-escalation is conditional on clinical and radiological stability
- Lab monitoring (IgG, CD19+ B cells) drives dosing decisions
- Age and risk group status modulate de-escalation pace
- Treatment can be restarted if disease re-emerges after cessation
