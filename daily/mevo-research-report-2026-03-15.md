## Executive Summary

Medium vessel occlusions (MeVOs) account for **25-40% of all acute ischemic strokes** yet remain a therapeutic gray zone. Three major RCTs (ESCAPE-MeVO, DISTAL, and REACT) have now reported **neutral or futile** results for endovascular thrombectomy (EVT) vs. best medical management. However, post-hoc subgroup analyses and observational data suggest **specific enriched populations** may still benefit. The field is pivoting toward patient selection refinement, device-vessel size matching, and penumbral salvage optimization.

---

## 1. What Are MeVOs?

- **Definition:** Occlusions of M2 MCA, A2 ACA, P2 PCA, and their distal branches (typically 1.5-2.5 mm diameter)
- **Prevalence:** 25-40% of all acute ischemic strokes
- **Clinical significance:** Substantial morbidity despite best medical management; often cause significant functional deficits

## 2. Key RCT Results (2025-2026)

### ESCAPE-MeVO Trial (NEJM 2025)
- **Reference:** Goyal M, Ospel JM, Ganesh A, et al. *N Engl J Med.* 2025;392:1385-1395. PMID: 39908448
- **Design:** RCT, MeVO stroke randomized to EVT + usual care vs. usual care alone
- **Result:** **No significant benefit** of EVT over usual care for 90-day mRS
- **Key finding:** 52.2% of EVT patients achieved meTICI 2c-3 reperfusion, but even these patients showed no significant mRS improvement vs. usual care (aOR 1.17, 95% CI 0.79-1.75) [PMID: 41651659]

### DISTAL Trial (NEJM 2025)
- **Reference:** Psychogios M, Brehm A, Ribo M, et al. *N Engl J Med.* 2025;392:1374-1384. PMID: 39908430
- **Design:** RCT, EVT for medium/distal vessel occlusion
- **Result:** **Neutral** — no significant improvement in functional outcomes

### REACT Trial
- Interim analysis presented; results consistent with ESCAPE-MeVO and DISTAL
- Fourth trial expected to report soon

### Canadian Stroke Best Practice Recommendations (Interim Update 2025)
- **Reference:** Heran MKS, et al. *Can J Neurol Sci.* 2025. PMID: 41340372
- **Guidance:** Updated recommendations address MeVO specifically, acknowledging new RCT evidence

## 3. Post-Hoc and Observational Insights

### 3.1 Enrichment Strategies for MeVO EVT (Cendrero et al., JNIS 2026)
- **Reference:** PMID: 41638899
- **Cohort:** 232 EVT-treated MeVO patients, 2 centers
- **Recanalization rate:** 84.9%
- **Overall treatment effect:** +33.3% functional independence (recanalized 53.3% vs. non-recanalized 20.0%)
- **Enrichment strategies that amplified benefit:**
  - Age ≤80 years (+13.8%)
  - AI-predicted infarct core ≤9 mL (+9.9%)
  - NIHSS ≥10 (+6.7%)
- **Combined enrichment:** Treatment effect increased to **61.9%** (added value +33.6%)
- **Takeaway:** Pragmatic imaging + clinical enrichment can identify MeVO patients who benefit substantially from EVT

### 3.2 Penumbral Salvage vs. Infarct Volume Reduction (He et al., ESJ 2026)
- **Reference:** PMID: 41614499
- **Cohort:** 338 MeVO patients, multicenter, 2020-2024
- **Successful reperfusion rate:** 72%
- **Median follow-up infarct volume (FIV):** 21 mL (IQR 12-32 mL)
- **Median penumbra salvage index (PSI):** 0.68 (IQR 0.50-0.82)
- **Key finding:** Penumbral salvage accounted for **44%** of reperfusion's effect on excellent outcomes (mRS 0-1), vs. only 16% for infarct volume reduction
- **Successful reperfusion → excellent outcome:** aOR 1.92 (95% CI 1.08-3.47, p=0.020)
- **Takeaway:** Penumbral salvage, not just infarct size reduction, is the dominant mechanism of EVT benefit in MeVO

### 3.3 Device-Vessel Size Mismatch (Virtanen et al., ESJ 2026)
- **Reference:** PMID: 41614530
- **Cohort:** 146 M2 occlusion EVT patients
- **Critical finding:** When thrombectomy device is >20% larger than the occluded vessel (ratio >1.2):
  - **sICH rate:** 20.0% (vs. 7.0% when device ≤ vessel size)
  - **Futility rate (mRS 5-6):** 34.2% (vs. 17.3%)
  - **Adjusted OR for sICH:** 3.32 (1.04-10.64)
  - **Adjusted OR for futility:** 2.84 (1.09-7.37)
- **Takeaway:** Oversized devices in small vessels are a modifiable cause of harm — size-matching is critical

### 3.4 Frontline Aspiration vs. Stent Retriever for M2 (STAR Registry, ESJ 2026)
- **Reference:** PMID: 41614461
- **Data from:** STAR (Stroke Thrombectomy and Aneurysm Registry)
- **Context:** Most MeVO trial participants received stent retrievers as first attempt
- **Finding:** Frontline aspiration showed different technical profiles; may be preferable for smaller vessels
- **Takeaway:** Technique selection matters — aspiration may be safer for distal targets

### 3.5 Primary vs. Rescue MeVO (Ozdemir et al., J Clin Med 2025)
- **Reference:** PMID: 41303045
- **Key distinction:** Primary MeVO (de novo) vs. Rescue MeVO (after LVO thrombectomy)
- **Finding:** These are heterogeneous populations with different clinical trajectories; trial results should not be uniformly applied

### 3.6 Comorbidity Burden and MeVO EVT Outcomes (Hall et al., SVIN 2025)
- **Reference:** PMID: 41573177
- **Design:** Nationwide prospective observational study (Sweden)
- **Finding:** High comorbidity burden may attenuate EVT benefit — trial-underrepresented populations behave differently

## 4. The ESCAPE-MeVO Reperfusion Subanalysis (JNIS 2026)

**Reference:** Diprose W, et al. *J Neurointerv Surg.* 2026. PMID: 41651659

Even achieving meTICI 2c-3 reperfusion in ESCAPE-MeVO did NOT significantly improve 90-day mRS vs. usual care:
- 253/255 EVT patients had final meTICI scores
- 133 (52.2%) achieved meTICI 2c-3
- Infarct volumes were lower in meTICI 2c-3 group
- BUT: adjusted common OR for mRS improvement = 1.17 (0.79-1.75) — not significant

**Interpretation:** Good reperfusion alone is insufficient. Patient selection (who has meaningful penumbra to salvage) matters more than achieving technical success.

## 5. State-of-the-Art Review (Diprose et al., Stroke 2026)

**Reference:** Diprose WK, et al. *Stroke.* 2026;57(3):814-828. PMID: 41235430

**Key points:**
- MeVO = 25-40% of AIS; associated with substantial morbidity
- Three published/presented trials suggest **no difference** between EVT and BMM; **possibility of harm**
- Promising subgroups identified via post-hoc analyses
- Future directions:
  - Improve EVT tools/techniques for small vessels
  - Adjunctive treatments to increase technical efficacy
  - Refined imaging-based patient selection
  - Focus future trials on enriched subgroups

## 6. Clinical Implications & Path Forward

### What We Know:
1. **Unselected MeVO EVT does not improve outcomes** (3 RCTs concordant)
2. **Device-vessel size mismatch causes harm** — use appropriately sized devices
3. **Penumbral salvage is the key mechanism** — not just infarct volume reduction
4. **Enrichment works:** Age ≤80, NIHSS ≥10, small predicted core → 61.9% treatment effect

### What's Next:
1. **Next-generation devices** designed for 1.5-2.5 mm vessels
2. **AI-based imaging selection** (e.g., Methinks AI-ICV for core prediction)
3. **Enriched RCT designs** targeting the subgroups identified
4. **Aspiration-first strategies** for distal occlusions
5. **Combination approaches** (EVT + neuroprotectants)
