# SaaS Product Ideas — AI Education & Medical/Neurology
**Date:** 2026-04-05
**Source:** Gap analysis + market research

---

## Top 2 Ideas

### Idea 1: AI Cognitive Screening Co-pilot for Primary Care
**Problem:** Primary care physicians (PCPs) are the front line for dementia/MCI detection but lack time, tools, and reimbursement pathways for proper cognitive assessment — yet new amyloid-targeting drugs (lecanemab, donanemab) make early detection commercially critical. Current options: expensive neuropsychological batteries (only in specialist settings) or 2-minute MMSE that's insufficient and unreimbursed at scale.

**Target User:** Independent primary care practices (1-5 physicians), community health centers.

**MVP Approach:** A tablet-based SaaS that delivers a 10-minute AI-guided cognitive battery (digit span, clock drawing, verbal fluency, picture naming) in the exam room, auto-generates a reimbursable note, and flags high-risk patients for specialist referral — priced at $40-80/month per provider.

---

### Idea 2: Differentiated Worksheet Generator for Teachers
**Problem:** Teachers spend 10-15 hours/week creating individualized materials for students at different mastery levels, yet almost no AI edtech focuses on this specific pain point — the output side of personalization. Existing tools (Khanmigo, Carnegie) are full platforms requiring curriculum lock-in.

**Target User:** Individual teachers and small tutoring centers (grades 4-12), especially in under-resourced public schools.

**MVP Approach:** A web app where a teacher pastes any lesson content or worksheet and with one click gets 3-5 versions at different difficulty tiers, aligned to the same learning standard — free tier with 20 worksheets/month, $12/month for unlimited.

## Runner-Up Ideas

### Idea 3: Ambient Documentation for Neurology Practices
Neurologists face some of the most complex documentation (neuro exam findings, imaging reports, longitudinal progression tracking) yet ambient scribing tools like Nabla/DaVinci are generalist. A neurology-specialized ambient AI that understands EEG order sets, neuro exam terminology, and CMT/ALS progression scoring could command a premium.

### Idea 4: AI Learning Companion for Adult Vocational Learners
Gap between AI tutoring (Khanmigo, Duolingo — K-12/consumer) and enterprise L&D (LinkedIn Learning, Docebo — top-down). Adult vocational learners (trades, healthcare aides, tech bootcamps) need AI that adapts to their job-linked skill gaps and schedules. A SaaS bundled with community college or apprenticeship programs could capture this underserved segment.

### Idea 5: Remote Neurodegenerative Monitoring SaaS
Neurologists managing Parkinson's, MS, and dementia patients have no good tool to track patients between visits. Patients self-report; clinicians guess. A smartphone-based weekly check-in (tremor analysis via accelerometer, gait via camera, cognitive via speech) with AI trend alerts to the neurologist — billable as remote therapeutic monitoring (RTM) codes.
