# Electrophysiology Notes

## Hypoglossal Nerve Conduction
- **Study:** Oliveira AL et al. Muscle Nerve. 2026 Mar;73(3):427-433. PMID: 41416495
- **Method:** Novel submental electrode placement (active) vs traditional tongue electrodes
- **N=34 healthy adults**
- **CMAP findings:**
  - Onset latency: mean 1.27 ms, upper limit 1.58 ms
  - Amplitude: >0.88 mV
  - Decremental response: -0.53% (minimal)
- **Advantage:** Eliminates saliva interference, no specialized tongue electrodes needed, good reproducibility
- **Clinical use:** Hypoglossal NCS for bulbar dysfunction assessment

## Phrenic Nerve Conduction — Normative Data
- **Study:** Castro J, de Carvalho M. J Clin Neurophysiol. 2026 Feb;43(2):176-182. PMID: 40554516
- **N=132 healthy subjects (age 23–90) — largest dataset to date**
- **Key findings:**
  - Latency increases ~0.25 ms/decade with age
  - Men have higher amplitudes than women
- **Abnormal thresholds:**
  - Men: latency >9.5 ms or amplitude <0.62 mV
  - Women: latency >8.5 ms or amplitude <0.48 mV
  - Left-sided CMAP amplitudes significantly larger
- **Clinical use:** Assessing diaphragm compromise in neuromuscular diseases (ALS, Guillain-Barré, etc.)
