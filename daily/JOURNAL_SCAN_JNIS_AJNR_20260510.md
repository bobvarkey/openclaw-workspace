## JNIS — Journal of NeuroInterventional Surgery

### 1. Stent Retriever vs. Aspiration Thrombectomy: Nationwide Registry
- **Journal:** JNIS, Vol 17, e2/e245
- **What it covers:** Nationwide registry-based cohort study comparing stent retriever versus aspiration-first thrombectomy for large vessel occlusion. Endpoints: first-pass reperfusion, procedure time, clinical outcomes.
- **Why it matters:** One of the largest real-world comparisons of the two dominant thrombectomy techniques. Results directly inform first-line device selection in everyday practice.

### 2. Proceduralist Volume & Thrombectomy Mortality
- **Journal:** JNIS, Vol 17, No 11, pp 1185
- **What it covers:** Analysis linking higher individual operator thrombectomy volume to reduced inpatient mortality after mechanical thrombectomy.
- **Why it matters:** Provides evidence for threshold-volume requirements in stroke center credentialing and supports regionalization of care.

### 3. Cangrelor vs. GPIIb/IIIa Inhibitors as Adjunctive Therapy in LVO Stroke
- **Journal:** JNIS, Online First (jnis-2025-023260)
- **What it covers:** Comparison of cangrelor (IV P2Y12 inhibitor) versus traditional GPIIb/IIIa inhibitors during endovascular treatment for LVO stroke.
- **Why it matters:** Cangrelor's rapid onset/offset profile is attractive in acute stroke. This head-to-head informs periprocedural antiplatelet strategy, especially in rescue stenting scenarios.

### 4. Regional Brain Atrophy, White Matter Lesions & Functional Outcome After EVT
- **Journal:** JNIS, jnis-2026-024941 (Mar 27, 2026)
- **What it covers:** Pre-existing regional brain atrophy and white matter disease burden as predictors of functional outcome after endovascular thrombectomy for AIS.
- **Why it matters:** Helps identify patients unlikely to benefit despite successful recanalization — clinically useful for prognostication and consent discussions.

### 5. Flow Diversion with Hydrophilic Polymer Coating + Prasugrel SAPT for Ruptured Aneurysms
- **Journal:** JNIS, Vol 17, No 8, pp 870
- **What it covers:** Multicenter case series of flow diversion using hydrophilic polymer-coated (Shield) devices with prasugrel single antiplatelet therapy in acutely ruptured aneurysms.
- **Why it matters:** Ruptured aneurysm flow diversion remains high-risk; this combination may reduce hemorrhagic complications from dual antiplatelet therapy while maintaining thromboembolic protection.

### 6. Nomogram for Delayed Intraparenchymal Hemorrhage After Pipeline Embolization
- **Journal:** JNIS, Vol 18, No 3, pp 725
- **What it covers:** Multicenter development and validation of a nomogram predicting delayed parenchymal hemorrhage after Pipeline flow diversion.
- **Why it matters:** A practical risk-stratification tool for post-procedural monitoring and antiplatelet management.

## AJNR — American Journal of Neuroradiology

### 7. EVT vs. Medical Management for LVO Stroke with Brain Malignancy
- **Journal:** AJNR, ajnr.A9359 (published online Apr 20, 2026)
- **What it covers:** Comparison of endovascular thrombectomy vs. medical management for LVO stroke in patients with known brain malignancy.
- **Why it matters:** Critically understudied population — cancer patients are often excluded from trials. Addresses whether thrombectomy benefits extend to patients with intracranial tumors.

### 8. Robotic Intracranial Navigation Using a Magnetically Steerable Guidewire
- **Journal:** AJNR, ajnr.A9361 (published online Apr 21, 2026)
- **What it covers:** In-vitro feasibility study of a magnetically steerable guidewire for intracranial navigation, with performance benchmarking against standard devices for stroke treatment.
- **Why it matters:** Represents a significant step toward robotic-assisted neurointervention — could improve navigation in tortuous anatomy and reduce radiation exposure.

### 9. "ↃC Stenting" for Wide-Neck Basilar Apex Aneurysms
- **Journal:** AJNR, ajnr.A9367 (published online Apr 25, 2026)
- **What it covers:** Clinical case series and technical note on a novel "reverse C" stenting technique for wide-neck basilar apex aneurysms.
- **Why it matters:** Offers a new reconstructive option for a challenging aneurysm location where conventional Y-stenting may be suboptimal.

### 10. Chemical vs. Chemical + Mechanical Angioplasty for Cerebral Vasospasm After aSAH
- **Journal:** AJNR, ajnr.A9194 (published online Mar 27, 2026)
- **What it covers:** Pilot randomized controlled study comparing standalone chemical angioplasty to combined chemical + mechanical angioplasty for vasospasm after aneurysmal SAH.
- **Why it matters:** The first RCT data on this comparison — informs management of a common and devastating complication of subarachnoid hemorrhage.

### 11. Tenecteplase vs. Alteplase as Bridging Thrombolysis Before MT for Medium/Distal Vessel Occlusions
- **Journal:** AJNR, ajnr.A9219 (published online Feb 9, 2026)
- **What it covers:** Comparison of TNK vs. alteplase as bridging therapy before mechanical thrombectomy specifically for medium and distal vessel occlusions (MeVOs/DiVOs).
- **Why it matters:** Extends the TNK vs. alteplase debate into the MeVOs/DiVOs space — increasingly relevant as these occlusions gain attention in trials and practice.

### 12. Ticagrelor vs. Clopidogrel for Flow Diversion of Unruptured Aneurysms
- **Journal:** AJNR, ajnr.A9257 (published online Feb 23, 2026)
- **What it covers:** Multicenter investigation comparing ticagrelor with clopidogrel for patients undergoing elective flow diversion for unruptured intracranial aneurysms.
- **Why it matters:** Addresses the optimal antiplatelet regimen for flow diversion — clopidogrel non-responder issue vs. cost/side-effect profile of ticagrelor.

### 13. Cost-Effectiveness of MMA Embolization for cSDH (EMBOLISE Secondary Analysis)
- **Journal:** AJNR, ajnr.A9319 (published online Mar 27, 2026)
- **What it covers:** Health-economic analysis from the EMBOLISE trial — cost-effectiveness of adjunctive middle meningeal artery embolization for chronic subdural hematoma.
- **Why it matters:** With MMA embolization now proven effective, cost-effectiveness data are critical for guideline inclusion and payer coverage decisions.

## INR — Interventional Neuroradiology

### 14. Flow Diversion for Acutely Ruptured Intracranial Aneurysms: Single-Center 30-Case Series
- **Journal:** Interventional Neuroradiology (via PubMed)
- **What it covers:** Retrospective analysis of flow diversion as acute treatment for ruptured intracranial aneurysms.
- **Why it matters:** Complements the JNIS polymer-coated flow diverter series — adds safety/efficacy data to the evolving practice of using flow diverters in the ruptured setting.

### 15. Aspiration vs. Stent-Retriever as First-Line Therapy for Primary Medium/Distal Occlusions
- **Journal:** Interventional Neuroradiology (via PubMed)
- **What it covers:** Propensity-score matched multicenter analysis comparing aspiration-first vs. stent-retriever-first technique for medium and distal vessel occlusions.
- **Why it matters:** As distal/medium vessel occlusions become a focus of thrombectomy expansion, this provides technique-specific evidence for this vessel caliber.
