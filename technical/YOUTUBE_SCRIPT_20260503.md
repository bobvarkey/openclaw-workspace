## SCRIPT

### Section 1: What Is a Flow Diverter? (0:30 - 2:00)
- A mesh tube stent placed inside the artery to divert blood flow away from aneurysm
- Unlike coiling (fills aneurysm from inside) or clipping (clamps base), flow diversion addresses the parent vessel wall
- Main devices: Pipeline Embolization Device (Medtronic), Surpass Evolve (Stryker), FRED (Terumo)
- Think of it like rerouting a river — you don't dam the lake, you redirect the current

### Section 2: The 5-Year Evidence (2:00 - 6:00)

**SCENT Trial — Surpass Flow Diverter**
- 5-year results published 2026
- Long-term safety: sustained, with no late complications emerging
- Effectiveness: aneurysm occlusion rates continued improving over 5 years — >85% complete occlusion
- Key insight: once the aneurysm heals, it stays healed. Late recurrence is rare.

**Pipeline FOREVER Trial (Internal Carotid Artery Aneurysms)**
- 5-year data: mRS 0-2 in 94%+ of patients at last follow-up
- Major complication rate: <3% — lower than surgical clipping for complex ICA aneurysms
- The caveat: these are centers with high volume operators. Results don't generalize as well to lower-volume centers.

**FRED Flow Diverter — 5-Year Efficacy**
- 5-year occlusion rates: ~80% complete occlusion
- Safety profile: consistent with other flow diverters
- Key paper: Stroke: Vascular and Interventional Neurology, 2026

**MCA M1 Aneurysms (Frontiers 2026)**
- Small series but important: 94.3% mRS 0-2 at median 13 months
- Shows flow diversion works even in challenging locations

### Section 3: Who Should Actually Get One? (6:00 - 8:00)
- **Best candidates:**
  - Large/giant aneurysms where coiling fails
  - Internal carotid artery aneurysms (best evidence base)
  - Fusiform aneurysms (no neck to clip or coil)
  - Patients with high surgical risk

- **When it fails:**
  - Very small parent vessels (<2mm) — harder to deploy
  - Aneurysms with incorporated branches — flow diverters can block side branches
  - When dual antiplatelet therapy is contraindicated

### Section 4: The Antiplatelet Problem (8:00 - 9:00)
- You need dual antiplatelet therapy (aspirin + clopidogrel) for at least 6 months
- For neurointerventionists: this is the biggest practical barrier
- Patients who can't tolerate DAPT: surgical clipping or coiling may still be better
- Newer devices exploring bioresorbable options — early data from 2026 JNIS paper on magnesium BRFD

### Section 5: Bottom Line (9:00 - 10:00)
1. Flow diverters have 5-year data proving long-term durability
2. Best for complex, large, or fusiform aneurysms in high-volume centers
3. The antiplatelet requirement is the main practical constraint
4. Outcomes depend heavily on operator experience
5. This is not a "better coiling" — it's a fundamentally different treatment paradigm
