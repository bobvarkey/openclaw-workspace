# X Thread — Thrombectomy: The 24-Hour Window (2026-04-07)
# Status: DRAFT — READY TO POLISH

## Thread Summary
Evidence that stroke thrombectomy extends to 24 hours, who benefits, and why it changes everything.

---

## Tweet 1/7 ⚡
The 6-hour stroke window is a myth.

DAWN trial (N=206): Thrombectomy 6-24 hours after last-known-well.
Results: 49% functional independence vs 13% controls.
NNT = 3.

Every 3 patients treated, 1 extra person walks out of hospital.

This wasn't a marginal result. It was a 36-point absolute difference.

---

## Tweet 2/7 🧠
Why does the window extend?

It's not the clock. It's the tissue.

CT perfusion identifies:
- Core infarct (already dead tissue)
- Penumbra (salvageable tissue)

As long as penumbra exists → the patient can benefit.

The window is tissue-based, not time-based.

---

## Tweet 3/7 📊
DEFUSE 3 confirmed it (6-16 hour window):
- 44% functional independence (thrombectomy)
- 16% controls
- NNT: 3.6

Same result, different population.

Both trials used perfusion imaging as the gatekeeper, not the clock.

---

## Tweet 4/7 😴
Wake-up stroke is the biggest opportunity.

20-25% of all strokes are wake-up strokes.
Traditionally → excluded from thrombolysis and thrombectomy.

DAWN/DEFUSE 3 included wake-up patients.

If you wake up with a new deficit → get a CT perfusion.
Don't assume it's 'too late.'

---

## Tweet 5/7 🎯
Who actually benefits (6-24 hour window)?

✅ Large vessel occlusion (ICA, M1, sometimes M2)
✅ Core infarct < 70mL on perfusion
✅ Favorable penumbra-to-core ratio (>1.8)
✅ NIHSS typically ≥ 6

❌ Large established infarcts (>100mL core)
❌ Posterior circulation — less robust data

---

## Tweet 6/7 ⚠️
Common mistakes I see:

1. "Outside the window" → reflexively declining thrombectomy
2. Getting a non-contrast CT only → missing perfusion opportunity  
3. Delaying transfer because "too much time has passed"
4. Ignoring wake-up strokes in the ED

Every stroke patient deserves a perfusion study.

---

## Tweet 7/7 💡
Bottom line:

Time is still brain.
But "time" now means tissue, not hours.

6 hours was a surrogate marker for salvageable tissue.
Perfusion imaging is the actual marker.

The window didn't open.
We just learned to measure the right thing.
