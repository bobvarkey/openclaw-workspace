## SECTION 2: The DAWN Trial (3:00)

**[Graphics + voiceover]**

"In 2018, the DAWN trial dropped a bomb.

We randomized wake-up stroke and late-presenting stroke patients (6-24 hours) to thrombectomy + standard care vs. standard care alone.

The results:
- 49% of thrombectomy patients achieved functional independence (mRS 0-2)
- vs. 13% in the control group
- NNT of just 3

**Three patients treated. One extra person walking out of hospital.**

This wasn't marginal. This was a 36-point absolute difference in disability-free survival."

**[Key visual: DAWN trial results graphic — mRS distribution chart]**

---

## SECTION 3: DEFUSE 3 (5:00)

"DEFUSE 3 confirmed it.

Extended window thrombectomy (6-16 hours) in patients with favorable perfusion imaging:
- 44% functional independence in thrombectomy group
- 16% in control
- NNT: 3.6

Both trials used CT perfusion and MRI DWI to identify patients with salvageable penumbra — the tissue that's already lost its blood flow but hasn't died yet.

**The key insight: It's not the clock that matters. It's the tissue.**"

---

## SECTION 4: Who Benefits? (8:00)

**[On camera — showing imaging]**

"Based on DAWN + DEFUSE 3, here's the modern selection criteria:

**For 6-24 hour window:**
✅ Large vessel occlusion (ICA, M1, M2)
✅ Favorable perfusion: core infarct < 70mL
✅ Penumbra-to-core ratio > 1.8
✅ Clinical deficit correlating to imaging

**Who DOESN'T benefit:**
❌ Large established infarcts (> 70-100mL core)
❌ NIHSS < 6 (mild strokes — consider risks carefully)
❌ Posterior circulation — data less robust

## SECTION 5: Wake-Up Stroke — The Biggest Opportunity (10:00)

"Here's the population everyone ignores: wake-up stroke.

You go to bed normal. You wake up with a deficit.

Traditionally? MRI was required, tPA was questionable, thrombectomy was off the table.

**But wake-up stroke is 20-25% of all strokes.**

And DAWN/DEFUSE 3 included wake-up patients.

If you wake up with a new neurological deficit:
1. Get CT perfusion immediately
2. If mismatch profile is favorable → thrombectomy is on the table
3. tPA? Look at DWI-FLAIR mismatch on MRI

Don't assume it's 'too late.' Assume the tissue until proven otherwise."

## SECTION 6: Real-World Implementation (13:00)

**[On camera — practical/talk-style]**

"Let me be honest about the system challenges:

1. **Imaging requirements**: CT perfusion isn't available at every center 24/7. Know your hospital's capabilities.

2. **Inter-facility transfer**: If you're at a center without thrombectomy capability, DAWN-eligible patients should be transferred. Every hour matters — but the 24-hour ceiling means transfer is more often justified.

3. **Neurologist gatekeeping**: Some ED physicians still reflexively say 'outside the window.' Patient advocates need to know this is wrong.

4. **Reimbursement**: This varies by country. Know your local stroke pathway.
