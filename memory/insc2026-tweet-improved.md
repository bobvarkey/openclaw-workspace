## Option 2 (Educational thread)

🧵 **The 9-hour midpoint strategy for wake-up strokes (INSC 2026)**

The problem: 8-30% of ischemic strokes happen during sleep. Unknown onset = often disqualified from treatment.

The fix: Use midpoint between sleep and wake as proxy for timing.

Why 9 hours works:
• Most wake-up strokes occur near waking (6AM–noon)
• Circadian surge: BP ↑, HR ↑, coagulation ↑
• DWI-FLAIR shows strokes are "physiologically young"
• Perfusion mismatch confirms salvageable penumbra

Treatment: rtPA or Tenecteplase beyond 4.5h if penumbra present.

This could save brain tissue otherwise lost.
