# INSC 2026 - Cancer-Associated Stroke (CAS)

**Source:** INSC 2026 Presentation
**Theme:** Stroke – Translating Research into Practice

---

## Slide 1: Key Clinical Phenotype

- Multiple territory infarcts ("three-territory sign")
- Markedly elevated/persistent D-dimer (>5.5 mg/L, specificity 99.7%)
- Active/metastatic cancer (pancreatic, lung, GI adenocarcinoma)
- Recurrent embolic stroke despite DOAC

---

## Slide 2: CAS vs Typical Stroke

| Mechanism | Non-Cardioembolic | Cancer-Associated |
|-----------|------------------|-------------------|
| Primary Driver | Atherothrombosis (platelet-rich) | Tumor hypercoagulability (TF→thrombin surge) |
| Biomarkers | CRP, lipids | Very high D-dimer, fibrinogen |
| Recurrence | Plaque instability | Ongoing tumor activity/NBTE |
| Therapy | Antiplatelets effective | Antiplatelets mechanistically limited |

---

## Slide 3: Why Antiplatelets & Warfarin Fail

**Antiplatelets:** Target platelet aggregation, miss thrombin-centric CAS cascade. Trials excluded cancer strokes (NAVIGATE ESUS)

**Warfarin:** Drug interactions with chemo, labile INR. Fails to suppress tumor-driven coagulopathy (OR 13x persistent D-dimer)

**→ Prioritize anticoagulation matching tumor biology**

---

## Slide 4: Treatment Hierarchy

**LMWH (Enoxaparin) – First Line:**
- Strongest evidence: suppresses thrombin generation
- Reduces D-dimer in cancer thrombosis
- Aligns with cancer-VTE guidelines

**DOACs – Second Line (Cautious):**
- Good for VTE, limited arterial CAS data
- Recurrence despite edoxaban reported

**Escalation:** IV UFH via chemo port (intolerant cases)

*Source: Jang HS et al., J Oncol 2015*

---

## Slide 5: Practical CAS Algorithm

```
Cancer Patient + Acute Stroke
 ↓
Step 1: Confirm CAS (multi-territory + high D-dimer + active Ca)
 ↓
Step 2: LMWH 3-6 months (tolerated)
 ↓ No/Recurrence
Step 3: DOAC → IV UFH → Thrombectomy
 ↓
Step 4: Duration = Cancer activity + D-dimer trend
```

*Reassess q3mo with Oncology*

---

## Slide 6: Duration & De-escalation Rules

| Continue LMWH if: | Stop/Switch if: |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| Active/metastatic Ca | Cancer remission |
| Ongoing chemo | Normalized D-dimer |
| Persistent high D-dimer | High bleed risk |
| NBTE or recurrence | No embolic events |

Most recurrences occur first 3 months – monitor closely

---

## Slide 7: Key Takeaways & Gaps

### Practice Now
✅ CAS = distinct entity (thrombin/NBTE-driven)
✅ LMWH > DOACs > antiplatelets for hypercoagulability
✅ Duration follows cancer activity/D-dimer

### Research Gaps
❌ No CAS-specific RCTs
❌ Optimal D-dimer thresholds unclear
❌ Adenocarcinoma/NBTE trials needed
