## Stroke: Translating Research into Practice

## Part I — Cancer-Associated Stroke (CAS)

### Clinical Phenotype of Cancer-Associated Stroke

**Characteristic Imaging:**
- Multiple territory infarcts
- Classic "three-territory sign"
- Often seen on diffusion MRI

**Laboratory Findings:**
- Markedly elevated D-dimer
- Often persistently elevated

**Clinical Context:**
- Active cancer
- Metastatic malignancy
- Recent chemotherapy

**Typical Stroke Classification:**
- Often categorized as **ESUS** — Embolic Stroke of Undetermined Source

**Pathology:**
- Platelet-rich thrombi
- Non-bacterial thrombotic endocarditis (NBTE)

**Common Associated Malignancies:**
- Pancreatic adenocarcinoma
- Lung cancer
- Gastrointestinal adenocarcinoma

**Clinical Course:**
- Recurrent strokes despite DOAC therapy

---

## Mechanistic Framework

### Tumor-Driven Hypercoagulability
Tumor cells release procoagulant factors:
- Tissue factor
- Cancer mucins
- Neutrophil extracellular traps (NETs)

These cause: Increased thrombin generation, platelet activation, systemic hypercoagulability.

### Platelet-Rich Microthrombosis
Microthrombi composed of platelets + fibrin occlude cerebral microvasculature.

### NBTE and Embolic Sources
- Sterile valvular vegetations
- Arterial emboli formation
- Occurs without infective endocarditis

### Treatment-Related Mechanisms
- Chemotherapy
- Radiation vasculopathy
- Anti-angiogenic drugs (Cisplatin, Bevacizumab)

---

## Diagnostic Clues for CAS

### D-dimer Levels by Stroke Type

| Stroke Type | D-dimer Level |
|-------------|---------------|
| Atherosclerotic | Mild elevation |
| Cardioembolic | Moderate |
| Cancer-related | Often very high (>3-5 µg/mL) |

**D-dimer Limitations:** Not specific - increases with infection, trauma, surgery, VTE, advanced cancer. Interpret with clinical context and imaging.

### Stroke Recurrence in Cancer
High D-dimer predicts: early recurrence, recurrent embolic strokes, systemic embolism, VTE. Persistent elevation = ongoing thrombogenesis.

## Part II — Anticoagulation Strategies

### Antiplatelet Therapy Limitations
Meta-analysis (Frontiers in Neurology 2026): Dual antiplatelet reduces recurrence in non-cardioembolic stroke, but trials excluded cancer-mediated stroke.

**Mechanistic Problem:** CAS is primarily driven by coagulation cascade + thrombin generation. Antiplatelets only target platelet aggregation → mechanistically inadequate.

**Clinical Takeaway:** Prefer anticoagulation aligned with tumor biology. Antiplatelets only when another clear indication exists.

### Warfarin: Historical Option
**Limitations:**
- Multiple chemotherapy interactions
- Labile INR
- Delayed onset
- Does not suppress tumor-driven thrombin generation effectively

**Evidence:** Warfarin-treated CAS patients often show persistently elevated D-dimer, minimal correction of coagulopathy.

### Direct Oral Anticoagulants (DOACs)
Strong evidence for cancer-associated VTE. Limited evidence for cancer-mediated arterial CAS. Recurrence despite edoxaban reported.

### Treatment Hierarchy
1. **LMWH (Enoxaparin) – First Line**
   - Strongest evidence: suppresses thrombin generation
   - Reduces D-dimer in cancer thrombosis
   - Aligns with cancer-VTE guidelines

2. **DOACs – Second Line (Cautious)**
   - Good for VTE, limited arterial CAS data
   - Recurrence despite edoxaban reported

3. **Escalation:** IV UFH via chemo port (intolerant cases)

## Part III — Practical CAS Algorithm

**Cancer Patient + Acute Stroke**
↓
**Step 1:** Confirm CAS (multi-territory + high D-dimer + active Ca)
↓
**Step 2:** LMWH 3-6 months (tolerated)
↓ No/Recurrence
**Step 3:** DOAC → IV UFH → Thrombectomy
↓
**Step 4:** Duration = Cancer activity + D-dimer trend
↓
*Reassess q3mo with Oncology*

---

## Part IV — Duration & De-escalation Rules

| Continue LMWH if: | Stop/Switch if: |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| Active/metastatic Ca | Cancer remission |
| Ongoing chemo | Normalized D-dimer |
| Persistent high D-dimer | High bleed risk |
| NBTE or recurrence | No embolic events |

Most recurrences occur first 3 months – monitor closely

---

## Part V — Key Takeaways & Research Gaps

### Practice Now
✅ CAS = distinct entity (thrombin/NBTE-driven)
✅ LMWH > DOACs > antiplatelets for hypercoagulability
✅ Duration follows cancer activity/D-dimer

### Research Gaps
❌ No CAS-specific RCTs
❌ Optimal D-dimer thresholds unclear
❌ Adenocarcinoma/NBTE trials needed
