# EVD Response Predicts Survival in Poor-Grade SAH

**Source:** Wang et al., J Neurosurg 2026 | Multicenter cohort, n = 1,589

---

## The Context

Among poor-grade aSAH patients who received preoperative EVD:

- **39%** showed clinical improvement after drainage
- **61%** did not improve

---

## Long-Term Mortality

| EVD Response | Mortality |
|--------------|-----------|
| Improved after EVD | **50%** |
| Did not improve | **97%** |

**47% DIFFERENCE** in mortality based on EVD response!

---

## Clinical Logic

### EVD Placed → GCS Improves
- **Injury likely pressure-driven** (reversible component)
- → **Better prognosis**

### EVD Placed → GCS Unchanged
- **Injury likely structural** (primary damage)
- → **Worse prognosis**

---

## Key Insight

**EVD response may be an underused early prognostic signal in the NeuroICU.**

If GCS improves after EVD placement → likely pressure-mediated injury with reversible component → better outcome.
If GCS unchanged → likely structural primary damage → poor outcome.

---

## Limitations

- Subgroup analysis: n = 50 with follow-up
- No multivariate adjustment
- Prospective validation needed
- **Hypothesis-generating, not practice-changing**
