### C. Direct Thrombectomy vs Bridging Therapy in AF-Related LVO
- **Source:** Frontiers in Neurology, 2026-03-13 | DOI: 10.3389/fneur.2026.1780191
- **Link:** https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fneur.2026.1780191
- **Study:** Retrospective, 221 AF-LVO patients (2020-2025), PSM analysis.
- **Key Findings:**
  - No significant difference in 90-day mRS 0-2: direct EVT 50% vs bridging 59% (p=0.158)
  - Direct EVT: shorter door-to-recanalization (125.5 vs 135.5 min, p=0.015), fewer passes (1 vs 2, p=0.003)
  - **Age ≥85 subgroup:** EVT alone significantly better — 50% vs 12.5% functional independence (p=0.041)
- **Takeaway:** In AF-related LVO, direct EVT comparable to bridging. In elderly (≥85), direct EVT may be preferred. Practice-changing potential for hyperacute triage.

- **Source:** JAMA Neurology 82(7):715-721, 2025 | PMID: 40455437 | DOI: 10.1001/jamaneurol.2025.1358
- **Link:** https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jamaneurology/fullarticle/2834602
- **Study:** Case-control, Finland, 245 FTD vs 2416 HCs vs 1326 AD patients (2010-2021).
- **Key Findings:**
  - Epilepsy prevalence 10 yrs before FTD dx: 3.3% (FTD) vs 0.8% (HCs) vs 1.4% (AD)

  - 5 yrs post-diagnosis: 11.2% (FTD) vs 2.2% (HCs) — difference 9.0 ppt (p<0.001)
  - ASM purchases: 10.2% (FTD) vs 1.8% (HCs) vs 4.2% (AD)
- **Takeaway:** Epilepsy significantly more prevalent in FTD than AD or controls. Seizures appear years before FTD diagnosis — potential early biomarker? Strong content angle.

## 3. AI / Machine Learning in Stroke

### F. ML Meta-Analysis: Pooled AUC 0.87 for AIS Prognosis
- **Source:** Frontiers in Neurology, 2026 | DOI: 10.3389/fneur.2026.1771341
- **Link:** https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fneur.2026.1771341
- **Study:** Systematic review + meta-analysis, 14 studies (12 in meta-analysis), PRISMA-compliant.
- **Key Findings:**
  - Pooled AUC: 0.87 (95% CI: 0.83-0.91) for ML models in AIS prognosis
  - Random Forest AUC 0.85, SVM AUC 0.82, XGBoost AUC 0.82 vs Logistic Regression AUC 0.75
  - Substantial heterogeneity (I2=99%) driven by study design, year, algorithm type
- **Takeaway:** ML consistently outperforms traditional LR for stroke outcome prediction. RF and XGBoost top performers. Heterogeneity remains a challenge for generalizability.
