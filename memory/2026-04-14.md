Based on the medical literature provided, here's a concise analysis of Alemtuzumab-induced Graves' disease and thyroidectomy:

**Key Findings:**
- Alemtuzumab (used for multiple sclerosis) can trigger severe autoimmune thyroid disease, including **Graves' disease** (hyperthyroidism) and **thyroid storm** (a life-threatening emergency).
- In rare but documented cases, **thyroidectomy** (removal of the thyroid gland) has been used as **secondary treatment** when:
  - Antithyroid medications fail
  - Thyroid storm develops (e.g., high fever, heart rhythm issues)
  - Case reports show successful resolution of symptoms post-surgery (e.g., [PMC 9138563](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9138563/) and [Cureus](https://www.cureus.com/articles/360811)).

**Important Notes:**
1. ⚠️ **This is NOT standard first-line treatment** – antithyroid drugs (like methimazole) are tried first.
2. 📉 **Risk/benefit balance** is critical: Thyroidectomy permanently removes the thyroid, requiring lifelong hormone replacement (levothyroxine).
3. 🩺 **Requires specialist evaluation** – Only endocrinologists with experience in autoimmune thyroid conditions would recommend this.

**Recommendation:**
If you're experiencing symptoms related to this condition, **seek immediate medical attention**. Thyroid storm is a medical emergency. For long-term management, consult an endocrinologist to discuss your specific case and all treatment options.

*Sources: PMC 9138563, Cureus case reports, MS Journal cohort study.*
