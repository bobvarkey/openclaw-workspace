# Shrimp Sign - Cerebellar PML

## Image Description

The "Shrimp Sign" is a characteristic finding seen on brain MRI (typically T2/FLAIR) in the dorsal brainstem/cerebellar region.

## Key Features

- **Appearance**: Curved, hyperintense (bright) lesion resembling a cooked shrimp
- **Location**: Dorsal medulla/area postrema, near floor of fourth ventricle
- **Also seen in**: Cerebellar involvement in PML (Progressive Multifocal Leukoencephalopathy)

## Clinical Significance

- **NMOSD**: Classic sign in Neuromyelitis Optica Spectrum Disorder
- **PML**: Can show cerebellar involvement with similar patterns
- Area postrema syndrome: intractable hiccups, nausea, vomiting

## Related Conditions

- Neuromyelitis Optica Spectrum Disorder (NMOSD)
- Progressive Multifocal Leukoencephalopathy (PML)
- Area postrema lesions
