# Colchicine in Secondary Prevention - Stroke & MACE

## Key Finding

**Low-dose colchicine** reduces ischemic stroke and Major Adverse Cardiovascular Events (MACE) in patients with:
- Prior stroke
- Coronary artery disease (ASCVD)

## Benefits

### Stroke Reduction
- ~6.6 strokes avoided per 1000 patients treated over 2 years
- Consistent treatment effect across key subgroups
- Non-cardioembolic ischemic stroke/TIA patients also benefit

### MACE Reduction  
- ~24 MACE events avoided per 1000 patients treated over 2 years

## Safety
- **No increase** in serious safety events
- **No increase** in death
- Well-tolerated at low doses

## Clinical Implications
- Colchicine is an anti-inflammatory agent
- Works via NLRP3 inflammasome inhibition
- Adds to secondary prevention armamentarium alongside:
  - Statins
  - Antiplatelets
  - ACEi/ARBs
  - Beta blockers

## Evidence
- Benefits seen in patients with established ASCVD
- Particularly effective in secondary prevention post-stroke/TIA
- Low-dose (0.5mg daily) is the studied regimen
