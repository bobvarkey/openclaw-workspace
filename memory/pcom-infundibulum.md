# PCOM Infundibulum vs Aneurysm - Expert Guidance

## Expert Opinion (Recorded: 2026-03-09)

**Key Management Principle:**
- **Early control imaging at 5-7 days**
- **If shape changes → Treat**
- **If shape stable → Observation**

## Summary: Infundibulum vs Aneurysm

### Infundibulum
- Funnel-shaped dilatation at PCOM origin
- Size: usually <3mm
- Shape: triangular/conical with apex pointing to PCOM
- No distinct neck
- **Generally benign** - not a cause of SAH

### True Aneurysm
- Focal saccular outpouching with distinct neck
- Size: usually >3mm
- Shape: saccular with dome
- **High rupture risk** - common cause of SAH (PCOM = ~30% of ruptured saccular aneurysms)

### Management in SAH
1. Look for other aneurysms as culprit
2. If PCOM lesion >3mm with distinct neck = likely culprit
3. Follow-up imaging at 5-7 days
4. If morphological change on follow-up = treat
