# Iron Absorption: Alternate-Day Dosing
Date added: 2026-04-04
Source: Clinical pharmacology literature

## Key Finding
Alternate-day iron supplementation outperforms daily dosing due to hepcidin-mediated ferroportin degradation.

## Mechanism
- **Dose ≥60 mg elemental iron** → liver releases **hepcidin**
- Hepcidin binds **ferroportin** (only iron export channel on intestinal enterocytes) → degrades it
- **Ferroportin degradation** → iron trapped in enterocyte, never reaches bloodstream
- Shutdown duration: ~24 hours
- Ferroportin restored by ~48 hours

## Evidence

### Stoffel et al. (2017) — Iron-depleted women (non-anemic)
| Schedule | Fractional Absorption | Total Iron Absorbed |
|----------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Alternate days | 21.8% per dose | 175 mg |
| Consecutive days | 16.3% per dose | 131 mg |

### 2019 Follow-up — Iron-deficient anemia women
- Fractional absorption **40–50% higher** on alternate days
- Significant because hepcidin is already partially suppressed by erythropoietic demand
- Shows hepcidin rebound still limits absorption even when body actively overriding it

## Caveats
- All studies used **ferrous sulfate**
- All studies in **women** — not tested in men
- Other iron forms (ferric, carbonyl, etc.) — effect unknown

## Clinical Implication
For iron repletion: **alternate-day dosing** maximizes absorption by respecting the hepcidin-ferroportin rhythm. Daily dosing triggers self-limiting hepcidin response that reduces overall iron uptake.
