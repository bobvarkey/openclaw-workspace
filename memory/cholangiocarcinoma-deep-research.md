# Cholangiocarcinoma (Biliary Tract Cancer) - Comprehensive Review

## Executive Summary

Cholangiocarcinoma (CCA) is a rare but aggressive malignancy arising from the biliary epithelium. The incidence is increasing globally, with a poor prognosis particularly in advanced stages. Recent advances in molecular profiling and targeted therapies have transformed the treatment landscape, with new approvals in 2024-2025 including durvalumab and zanidatamab. This review covers epidemiology, classification, diagnosis, staging, and current treatment paradigms.

---

## 1. Epidemiology and Risk Factors

### Incidence and Trends
- Global incidence: approximately 1-2 per 100,000 persons annually
- Rising incidence in Western countries over past decades
- Slight male predominance
- Peak incidence in 6th-7th decade of life

### Risk Factors
**Established:**
- Primary sclerosing cholangitis (PSC) - strongest risk factor
- Biliary cyst disease (choledochal cysts, Caroli's disease)
- Hepatolithiasis (bile duct stones)
- Chronic biliary inflammation (cirrhosis, hepatitis B/C)
- Diabetes and obesity
- Alcohol-related liver disease
- Genetic syndromes: Lynch syndrome, BAP1 tumor predisposition

**Possible:**
- Smoking
- Chronic pancreatitis
- Exposure to biliary irritants (e.g., dioxins)

## 2. Classification

Cholangiocarcinoma is classified anatomically into three main types:

### 2.1 Intrahepatic Cholangiocarcinoma (iCCA)
- Arises within the liver, from intrahepatic bile ductules to second-order bile ducts
- 10-20% of all CCA
- Often presents as liver mass with nonspecific symptoms
- Strong association with chronic liver disease and cirrhosis
- Molecular profile differs from extrahepatic CCA

### 2.2 Perihilar Cholangiocarcinoma (pCCA / Klatskin tumor)
- Arises at confluence of right and left hepatic ducts
- Most common type (50-60% of CCA)
- Presents with biliary obstruction (jaundice, pruritus)
- Requires complex surgical resection with biliary reconstruction

### 2.3 Distal Cholangiocarcinoma (dCCA)
- Arises below the cystic duct confluence to the ampulla of Vater
- 20-30% of CCA
- Often presents with obstructive jaundice
- Surgical treatment: pancreaticoduodenectomy (Whipple)

## 3. Diagnostic Approach

### 3.1 Imaging
**First-line:**
- **Ultrasound**: Initial evaluation of biliary dilation
- **CT abdomen**: Staging, vascular involvement, metastases
- **MRI/MRCP**: Gold standard for biliary anatomy, tumor extent

**Additional:**
- PET-CT: Detection of distant metastases
- Endoscopic ultrasound (EUS): Distal CCA, lymph nodes
- Cholangiopancreatography (ERCP/MRPCP): Biliary drainage + cytology

### 3.2 Biomarkers and Laboratory
- **CA 19-9**: Most widely used, not specific (also elevated in cholangitis, pancreatitis)
- **CEA**: Less specific, occasionally used
- **AFP**: To rule out hepatocellular carcinoma
- **Bilirubin, ALP, GGT**: Cholestatic pattern

### 3.3 Histopathological Confirmation
- Cytology (brushings, FNA) - limited sensitivity (~50%)
- Tissue biopsy (ERCP-guided, EUS-FNA, percutaneous)
- Molecular testing on tissue: Essential for treatment decisions

### 3.4 Staging Systems
- **TNM staging** (AJCC 8th edition): Different for iCCA vs eCCA
- **iCCA**: Liver-based staging
- **eCCA**: Perihilar vs distal有不同的 staging criteria
- **Liver Transplant Criteria** (Mayo Protocol): For unresectable pCCA

## 4. Treatment Paradigms

### 4.1 Surgical Treatment
**Curative Intent:**
- Complete surgical resection (R0) is only potentially curative
- R0 resection rates: 50-70% for iCCA, lower for pCCA
- Portal vein resection/reconstruction if involved
- Liver transplantation for select pCCA cases (Mayo Protocol)

**Adjuvant Therapy:**
- Capecitabine 6 months (BILCAP trial) - standard for R0/R1 resection
- Alternative: Gemcitabine + Cisplatin (for R1 margins, lymph node positive)
- Role of adjuvant immunotherapy under investigation

### 4.2 First-Line Systemic Therapy (Advanced Disease)

**Standard of Care (2024-2025):**
| Regimen | Evidence | Status |
|---------|----------|--------|
| **Gemcitabine + Cisplatin + Durvalumab** | TOPAZ-1 trial | First-line standard |
| Gemcitabine + Cisplatin | ABC-02 trial | Previous standard |

**TOPAZ-1 Trial Results:**
- Median OS: 12.9 months (vs 11.5 months with chemo alone)
- 2-year OS: 23.6% (vs 11.5%)
- 3-year OS update (2025): sustained benefit confirmed
- PD-L1 expression not required for benefit

### 4.3 Second-Line and Beyond

**Targeted Therapies (when molecular alterations present):**

| Target | Drug | FDA Status | Response Rate |
|--------|------|-------------|----------------|
| **FGFR2 fusions/rearrangements** | Pemigatinib | Approved 2020 | 36% ORR, 21 mo OS |
| **FGFR2 fusions** | Futibatinib | Approved 2022 | 42% ORR |
| **IDH1 mutations** | Ivosidenib | Approved 2021 | 32% ORR |
| **HER2+ (IHC 3+ or 2+)** | Zanidatamab | Approved Nov 2024 | 52% ORR |
| **HER2+** | Trastuzumab deruxtecan | Accelerated approval 2024 | >1/3 responders |

**Note:** Infigratinib was approved but FDA withdrew approval in May 2024.

**Chemotherapy Options:**
- FOLFOX: Second-line standard
- 5-FU/LV (Mayo Clinic regimen)
- Capecitabine monotherapy

**Immunotherapy:**
- Pembrolizumab: Approved for MSI-H/dMMR tumors (rare in CCA ~5%)
- Combination approaches under investigation

### 4.4 Locoregional Therapies
- **Transarterial Chemoembolization (TACE)**
- **Radioembolization (Y-90)**
- **Ablation** (microwave, radiofrequency)
- **Stereotactic Body Radiotherapy (SBRT)**
- For liver-dominant disease, bridging to transplant

## 5. Molecular Profiling and Precision Oncology

### 5.1 Key Molecular Alterations in CCA
| Alteration | Frequency | Therapeutic Implication |
|------------|-----------|------------------------|
| **FGFR2 fusions** | 10-15% (iCCA > pCCA) | Pemigatinib, Futibatinib |
| **IDH1 mutations** | 10-20% (iCCA) | Ivosidenib |
| **HER2 amplification** | 5-20% (higher in gallbladder) | Zanidatamab, Trastuzumab deruxtecan |
| **KRAS mutations** | 10-15% | Emerging: Adagrasib (ORR 42%) |
| **MSI-H/dMMR** | ~5% | Pembrolizumab |
| **BRAF V600E** | 1-5% | Dabrafenib + Trametinib (case reports) |
| **NTRK fusions** | <5% | Larotrectinib, Entrectinib |

### 5.2 Testing Recommendations
- **Next-generation sequencing (NGS)** is essential for all advanced CCA
- Test for: FGFR2, IDH1, HER2, KRAS, MSI, BRAF, NTRK
- Larger panels increasingly important as more targets emerge

## 6. Recent 2024-2025 Updates

### Key Developments

1. **Zanidatamab (Ziihera)** - FDA Approval Nov 2024
   - First HER2-targeted bispecific antibody for BTC
   - HERIZON-BTC-01 trial: 52% ORR, 14.9 month DOR
   - Accelerated approval for HER2+ biliary tract cancer

2. **Trastuzumab Deruxtecan (Enhertu)** - 2024
   - Tumor-agnostic accelerated approval for HER2+ solid tumors
   - Active in HER2+ BTC per Japanese studies

3. **Durvalumab + GemCis** - Updated 2025
   - 3-year OS data confirms sustained benefit
   - Now first-line standard globally

4. **Adagrasib in KRAS G12C CCA** - Promising
   - KRISTAL-1 trial: 41.7% ORR, 8.6 mo PFS, 15.1 mo OS
   - Not yet FDA approved for CCA but clinical trial enrollment encouraged

5. **EASL Guidelines 2025**
   - New Clinical Practice Guidelines for extrahepatic CCA (perihilar + distal)
   - Emphasizes molecular profiling, precision oncology
   - Structured diagnostic approach with multidisciplinary framework

6. **ESMO Guidelines Update 2025**
   - Comprehensive update addressing new developments
   - Focus on immunotherapy combinations and targeted therapy sequencing

## 7. Prognosis

### Survival by Stage
- **Localized (resectable)**: 5-year OS 30-50% (improved with adjuvant therapy)
- **Locally advanced**: Median OS 12-18 months
- **Metastatic**: Median OS 8-12 months (improved to ~13 months with durvalumab)

### Prognostic Factors
- Surgical margin status (R0 vs R1 vs R2)
- Lymph node involvement
- Tumor differentiation
- Perineural invasion
- CA 19-9 level (not prognostic alone, useful for monitoring)
- Molecular subtype (some FGFR fusions have better prognosis)

## 8. Emerging Therapies and Clinical Trials

### Active Research Areas
- Combination immunotherapy (durvalumab + CTLA-4, TKIs)
- FGFR inhibitor combinations
- KRAS G12C/G12D inhibitors
- ADC development beyond HER2
- Liver transplant protocols for unresectable pCCA
- Neoadjuvant therapy for resectable disease

### Key Ongoing Trials
- Several phase III trials evaluating second-line targeted combinations
- Neoadjuvant therapy trials for resectable CCA
- Combination of durvalumab with other agents

---

## 9. Summary and Clinical Takeaways

1. **Diagnosis**: Multidisciplinary approach with imaging, biopsy, and molecular testing
2. **Staging**: CT/MRI, consider PET, ensure adequate tissue for molecular profiling
3. **First-line**: Gemcitabine + Cisplatin + Durvalumab (new standard)
4. **Molecular Testing**: Essential - test for FGFR2, IDH1, HER2, MSI, KRAS
5. **Targeted Options**:
   - FGFR2 fusion: Pemigatinib, Futibatinib
   - IDH1 mutation: Ivosidenib
   - HER2+: Zanidatamab (new), Trastuzumab deruxtecan
6. **Second-line**: FOLFOX, consider targeted therapy if actionable mutation
7. **Follow Guidelines**: NCCN, ESMO, EASL 2025 updates
