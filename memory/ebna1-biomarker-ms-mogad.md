# EBNA-1 Antibody as Biomarker for Demyelinating Diseases

## Key Finding

**Persistent high-level EBNA-1 peptide antibody titers** may serve as a reliable biomarker for differentiating:
- **Multiple Sclerosis (MS)** from
- **MOGAD** (Myelin Oligodendrocyte Glycoprotein Antibody Disease) and
- **NMOSD** (Neuromyelitis Optica Spectrum Disorder)

## Clinical Utility

### Differential Diagnosis Challenge
MS, MOGAD, and NMOSD can present with similar demyelinating features:
- Optic neuritis
- Transverse myelitis
- Brain lesions

### EBNA-1 as Biomarker
- EBNA-1 (Epstein-Barr Nuclear Antigen-1) antibodies are elevated in MS
- Persistent high titers suggest MS rather than MOGAD or NMOSD
- Helps distinguish when imaging/clinical features are overlapping

## Clinical Application
- Use EBNA-1 antibody titers as adjunct in diagnostic workup
- High persistent levels → more likely MS
- Negative or low levels → consider MOGAD/NMOSD
