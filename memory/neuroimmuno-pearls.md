# Neuroimmunology Pearls - MS, MOGAD, NMOSD

## 🔬 OCT (Optical Coherence Tomography)

### Retinal Nerve Fiber Layer (pRNFL)
- **Acute pRNFL >118 μm** → think **MOGAD/NMOSD**, not MS
- **Inter-eye difference >20 μm** = red flag for **MOG/AQP4 disease**

### Clinical Application
- Thick pRNFL swelling in acute attack suggests antibody-mediated disease
- MS typically shows thinner pRNFL due to chronic neurodegeneration

---

## 🧲 Advanced MRI

### Central Vein Sign (CVS+) & PRL Lesions
- **≥6 CVS+ lesions** OR **≥1 PRL lesion** can rescue MS diagnosis when only ONE topography is involved

### PRL (Paramagnetic Rim Lesions)
- **PRL** = chronic active inflammation
- **Poor prognosis**
- **Half-life**: 7-10 years

### Clinical Application
- PRLs indicate smoldering inflammation
- Helps distinguish MS from mimics when dissemination in space is borderline

---

## MOGAD - Expanding Spectrum

MOGAD is bigger than just optic neuritis:

### Key Features
- **Seizures**: 10-19% (may **precede demyelination**!)
- **Isolated cranial neuropathy**: CN V most common
- **IIH with MOGAD** has been described

### Spectrum Expansion
MOGAD is increasingly recognized as a **multifocal disease** beyond just ON and myelitis.

## 💧 Biomarkers

| Biomarker | Clinical Use |
|-----------|-------------|
| **sNfL** (Neurofilament Light Chain) | Relapse activity marker in MS; **always correct for age & BMI** |
| **sGFAP** (Glial Fibrillary Acidic Protein) | Progression in MS & NMOSD relapse predictor |
| **κ-FLC** (Kappa Free Light Chains) | Automated, quantitative; **better alternative to OCB** |

### Additional Biomarker
- **EBNA-1 peptide antibodies**: Persistent high-level titers differentiate MS from MOGAD/NMOSD

---

## Summary: Red Flags for Non-MS

| Finding | Suggests |
|---------|----------|
| pRNFL >118 μm (acute) | MOGAD/NMOSD |
| Inter-eye difference >20 μm | MOG/AQP4 |
| Seizures with demyelination | MOGAD |
| Isolated cranial neuropathy | MOGAD |
| High EBNA-1 titers | MS (not MOGAD/NMOSD) |
