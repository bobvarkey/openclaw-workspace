# Alexander Disease — Research Update (March 2026)

## 🔴 MAJOR BREAKTHROUGH: Zilganersen — First Disease-Modifying Treatment on the Horizon

### FDA Priority Review Accepted (March 23, 2026)
- **Drug:** Zilganersen (ION373) by Ionis Pharmaceuticals
- **Target:** Glial fibrillary acidic protein (GFAP)
- **Mechanism:** Antisense oligonucleotide — inhibits excess GFAP production
- **PDUFA Date:** September 22, 2026
- **If approved:** First-ever disease-modifying treatment for Alexander disease

### Pivotal Study Results (NCT04849741)
- **Design:** Phase 1-3, randomized, double-blind, controlled, multiple ascending dose (MAD)
- **Participants:** 54 patients, ages 1.5–53 years, 13 sites in 8 countries
- **Dosing:** 50 mg every 12 weeks (subcutaneous)
- **Primary endpoint:** Gait speed (10-Meter Walk Test) at week 61
- **Result:** Statistically significant — 33.3% least square mean difference vs control (p=0.0412)
- **Safety:** Favorable safety and tolerability
- **Secondary endpoints (all favored zilganersen):** adaptive function, communication, GI symptoms, sleep, seizures

### Regulatory Designations
- ✅ Breakthrough Therapy Designation
- ✅ Orphan Drug Designation (FDA + EMA)
- ✅ Rare Pediatric Disease Designation
- ✅ Priority Review (granted March 2026)

### Future Data
- Additional pivotal study data to be presented at **2026 AAN Annual Meeting** in Chicago

## Background

### What is Alexander Disease?
- Rare, progressive, often fatal leukodystrophy
- Only known genetic disorder affecting astrocytes directly
- Caused by **autosomal dominant GFAP gene mutations** (chromosome 17q21)
- GFAP mutations = **gain-of-function** → abnormal protein aggregation → Rosenthal fibers → astrocyte dysfunction

### Epidemiology
- ~550 cases described worldwide (since first description in 1949)
- Japanese incidence: **1/2.7 million**
- ~1.6% of all leukodystrophies
- No sex predominance

### Types & Prognosis
| Type | Onset | Prognosis |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Neonatal | Birth | Severe disability/death within 2 years |
| Infantile | <2 years | Weeks to several years |
| Juvenile | 2–12 years | Variable; can reach 30+ years |
| Adult | >12 years | Slowest progression |

## Molecular Pathophysiology

### GFAP Biology
- Gene: 9 exons, 9.8 kb, encodes 432 amino acid intermediate filament protein
- Expressed in astrocytes; rapidly upregulated in reactive astrogliosis
- Functions: regulates astrocyte morphology, motility, and astrocyte-oligodendrocyte interactions

### Disease Mechanism
1. **GFAP mutation** → disruption of intermediate filament dimerization
2. **Protein aggregation** → Rosenthal fibers accumulate in astrocyte cytoplasm
3. **Focal accumulation** → proteasome impairment, cytoskeleton collapse
4. **Astrocyte dysfunction** → secondary damage to neurons, oligodendrocytes, microglia
5. **White matter destruction** → demyelination, cavitation, ventricular dilation

### Mutation Distribution (point mutations)
- Exon 1: **54%**
- Exon 4: **31%**
- Exon 8: 7%
- Exon 6: 4%
- Exon 5: 3%

### Key Insight: Gain-of-Function Disease
- GFAP-null mice show **low morbidity** — reduction of GFAP is well-tolerated
- No null variants found in human patients
- → Therapeutic window exists by reducing mutant GFAP expression

## Diagnosis

### Clinical Features
- Infantile: developmental regression, seizures, macrocephaly, spasticity, ataxia
- Juvenile/Adult: slower progression, milder symptoms

### MRI Findings
- White matter abnormalities — **frontal lobes** most characteristic
- Demyelination, cavitation
- Ventricular dilation
- Can involve temporal lobes, cerebellum, brainstem

### Genetic Testing
- Confirmatory: **GFAP gene sequencing**
- Identifies pathogenic variants

### Histopathology
- Rosenthal fibers (eosinophilic, radial arrangement around vessels and subpial/subependymal zones)
- Demyelination, vacuolation
- Progressive white matter destruction

## Treatment

### Current (Supportive)
- Seizure control (anticonvulsants)
- Physical therapy
- Supportive care

### Emerging: Antisense Oligonucleotide Therapy

#### Zilganersen (ION373)
- Binds to GFAP mRNA → inhibits excess protein production
- First-in-class RNA-targeted therapy for astrocyte disease
- Pivotal trial shows functional stabilization

#### Preclinical Evidence (mouse models)
- Single intracerebroventricular ASO injection in GFAP mutant mice
- ↓ Rosenthal fibers
- ↓ Astrocyte activation
- ↓ Microglia activation
- Proof-of-concept for antisense approach in Alexander disease

## Clinical Pearls

1. Alexander disease is the **only primary astrocyte disorder** known
2. GFAP mutations act via **gain-of-function** — reduction is therapeutic
3. **Rosenthal fibers** are the pathological hallmark
4. MRI white matter changes in **frontal lobes** are highly suggestive
5. **Seizures, macrocephaly, developmental regression** in infantile form
6. Consider in any unexplained leukodystrophy with GFAP mutation
7. Zilganersen could be **first approved therapy** by late 2026
