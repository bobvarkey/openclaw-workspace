DIAGNOSIS OF SECONDARY HYPERTENSION

Including Liddle Syndrome (Pseudo-hyperaldosteronism)
A-01
SECONDARY HTN — SCREENING
  • Age < 30 with hypertension, no risk factors
  • Resistant HTN (≥3 drugs including diuretic)
  • Accelerated / malignant hypertension
  • Sudden onset or worsening of HTN
  • Abdominal bruit or delayed femoral pulses
  • Hypokalemia (spontaneous or diuretic-induced)
  • Family history of early HTN or renal disease
B-01
CAUSES BY CATEGORY
🫘 RENAL Renovascular HTN (high renin/aldosterone, bruit) · Renal parenchymal disease
🧪 ENDOCRINE Conn's (low renin, HIGH aldo) · Pheochromocytoma · Cushing · Thyroid · HyperPTH
🫀 VASCULAR Coarctation (arm BP > leg, delayed femoral pulses, rib notching)
💤 SLEEP OSA (snoring, obesity, nocturnal hypoxia)
💊 DRUG-INDUCED NSAIDs · OCPs · Steroids · Decongestants · Licorice · EPO
🧬 MONOGENIC Liddle · Gordon · AME · GRA · CAH
C-01
⭐ LIDDLE SYNDROME — Pseudo-hyperaldosteronism

Autosomal dominant · Gain-of-function ENaC mutation (collecting duct)

Early-onset HTN Hypokalemic metabolic alkalosis No edema Family history present
KEY HALLMARK: ↓ LOW Renin + ↓ LOW Aldosterone (both suppressed!)

D-01
RENIN-ALDOSTERONE ALGORITHM
ReninAldosterone→ Diagnosis
↑ High ↑ High Renovascular HTN · Malignant HTN
↓ Low ↑↑ HIGH Primary hyperaldosteronism (Conn's)
↓ LOW ↓ LOW LIDDLE Syndrome · Gordon · AME · Licorice · CAH · Cushing
↔ Normal ↔ Normal Essential HTN · Renal parenchymal
💡 Clinical Pearl: Liddle is the only cause where BOTH renin AND aldosterone are LOW despite HTN and hypokalemia.
E-01
TREATMENT PEARLS
ConditionTreatment
Liddle Syndrome Amiloride / Triamterene (ENaC blockers)
Conn's Spironolactone / Eplerenone
Renovascular HTN Revascularization + ACEi/ARB
Pheochromocytoma α-blockade → β-blockade → surgery
OSA CPAP · Weight loss
Coarctation Surgical repair / Angioplasty
F-01
OTHER MONOGENIC HTN
  • Gordon syndrome — Hyperkalemic HTN, WNK mutations
  • AME (Apparent Mineralocorticoid Excess) — 11β-HSD2 deficiency. Licorice mimics this
  • GRA (Glucocorticoid-remediable Aldosteronism) — High aldosterone, responds to dexamethasone
  • CAH (11β-OH / 17α-OH deficiency)
G-01
