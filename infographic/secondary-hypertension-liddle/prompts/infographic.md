# Diagnosis of Secondary Hypertension — Including Liddle Syndrome

## SECTION 1: SECONDARY HTN — OVERVIEW
Screening triggers: Age<30 with HTN, resistant HTN (≥3 drugs), accelerated/malignant HTN, sudden onset, abdominal bruit, delayed femoral pulses, hypokalemia, family history of early HTN or renal disease.

## SECTION 2: CAUSES BY CATEGORY

### Renal
- Renovascular HTN (renal artery stenosis) — High renin, high aldosterone, unilateral bruit
- Renal parenchymal disease — Rising Cr, proteinuria, abnormal sediment

### Endocrine
- Primary hyperaldosteronism (Conn's) — Low renin, HIGH aldosterone. Treat: spironolactone
- Pheochromocytoma — Paroxysmal HTN, ↑ metanephrines. α-blockade→β-blockade→surgery
- Cushing syndrome — Cortisol excess, central obesity, striae
- Thyroid dysfunction — Hyper- or hypothyroid
- Hyperparathyroidism — ↑ calcium

### Vascular
- Coarctation of aorta — Higher BP in arms>legs, delayed femoral pulses, rib notching

### Sleep
- OSA — Daytime somnolence, snoring, obesity, nocturnal hypoxia

### Drug-induced
- NSAIDs, OCPs, steroids, decongestants, stimulants, licorice, EPO, cyclosporine

## SECTION 3: LIDDLE SYNDROME ⭐ FEATURED
Pseudo-hyperaldosteronism. Autosomal dominant, gain-of-function ENaC mutation (collecting duct).

Early-onset HTN (childhood/young adult), hypokalemic metabolic alkalosis, LOW renin AND LOW aldosterone ← KEY hallmark. No edema. Family history present.

Labs: ↓K+, ↑HCO₃⁻, ↓Renin, ↓Aldosterone, Normal-↑Na+, Normal AG
Urine: High Na+, High K+
Imaging: Usually normal (rule out other secondary causes)

Treatment: ✅ Amiloride or Triamterene (direct ENaC blockers)
❌ Spironolactone NOT effective (blocks aldosterone receptor; defect is downstream)

## SECTION 4: RENIN-ALDOSTERONE ALGORITHM

| Renin | Aldosterone | Diagnosis |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| ↑ High | ↑ High | Renovascular HTN, malignant HTN, reninoma |
| ↓ Low | ↑ HIGH | Primary hyperaldosteronism (Conn's) |
| ↓ Low | ↓ LOW | Liddle syndrome, Gordon, AME, licorice, CAH, Cushing |
| Normal | Normal | Essential HTN (dx of exclusion), renal parenchymal |

Clinical Pearl: Liddle is the only cause where BOTH renin AND aldosterone are LOW despite HTN and hypokalemia.

## SECTION 5: TREATMENT PEARLS

| Condition | Treatment |
|-----------|-----------|
| Liddle Syndrome | Amiloride / Triamterene (ENaC blockers) |
| Conn's | Spironolactone / Eplerenone |
| Renovascular HTN | Revascularization + ACEi/ARB |
| Pheochromocytoma | α-blockade → β-blockade → surgery |
| OSA | CPAP, weight loss |
| Coarctation | Surgical repair or angioplasty |

## SECTION 6: OTHER MONOGENIC HTN
- Gordon syndrome — Hyperkalemic HTN, WNK mutations
- AME — 11β-HSD2 deficiency. Licorice mimics
- GRA — Aldosterone high, responds to dexamethasone
- CAH — 11β-OH or 17α-OH deficiency
